## Affiliate Disclosure

Honest answer to: how does ExpatNetherlandsHub make money?

### How it works
When you click a button in an article like "View NordVPN" or "Try ProtonMail", you're redirected to the vendor's website. Some of those links are **affiliate links**. That means: if you subsequently buy a product or sign up for a service, the vendor pays us a commission. This costs YOU nothing extra — the price stays the same.

### Who we work with
We work with affiliate networks like Daisycon, AWIN, Impact.com and Direct (straight from the vendor). Concrete examples:
- VPN services (NordVPN, Surfshark)
- Encrypted email (ProtonMail, Tutanota)
- Password managers (NordPass, 1Password, Bitwarden)
- Privacy tools (Incogni, DeleteMe)

The complete list of partners is visible in our articles — every product link is clearly marked as an affiliate link via `rel="sponsored"`.

### What we DON'T do
- We do NOT write positive reviews for payment
- We do NOT accept guest articles or paid placements
- We do NOT hide affiliate links — all commercial links are clearly marked
- We do NOT receive extra commission for "better" reviews — commission is the same whether we give a product 5 stars or 2 stars

### How we stay independent
Our review ratings are based on:
1. Personal testing period (4-12 weeks hands-on)
2. Reader feedback (email correspondence with users)
3. Public data (prices, specs, transparency reports)
4. Comparison matrix with competitors

We have products we recommend alongside products where we say "don't buy this" — both pay us the same commission. That gives us the freedom to be honest.

### Legal framework
Affiliate disclosure is required under:
- **US**: FTC 16 CFR Part 255 (Endorsement Guides)
- **EU**: Digital Services Act (Article 26) — clear disclosure of paid communication
- **UK**: ASA / CAP Code

We comply via:
- Sticky disclosure text at top of every affiliate article
- `rel="sponsored noopener"` on all commercial links
- This page (linked from footer)

### What if I don't want to use an affiliate link?
No problem. Just type the product name into Google and go directly to their site. We'd prefer you use our link (you pay the same, we get the commission that funds our work), but you're entirely free to choose.
