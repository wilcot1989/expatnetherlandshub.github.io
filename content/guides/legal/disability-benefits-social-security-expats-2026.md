One of the most common questions I get from expats in their first year in the Netherlands is some version of: "If something goes wrong — I get sick, I lose my job, I can't work — what actually happens?" It is a reasonable thing to want to understand before you need it.

The Dutch social security system is, on balance, one of the more generous in Europe. It is also not simple. There are separate laws for short-term sickness, long-term disability, unemployment, and last-resort assistance, each with their own rules, timeframes, and contribution requirements. This guide walks through each one in practical terms — what it covers, what you get, and how to access it.

This matters for expats specifically because many arrive from countries with different systems and assume either that they are not covered (incorrect, if you work here and pay premiums), or that the process will be simple and quick (optimistic). Getting registered correctly from the start makes everything easier. Make sure you have completed your [first 30 days checklist](/guides/legal/first-30-days-netherlands-expat-checklist-2026/) and that your employment contract is properly documented — see [understanding your Dutch employment contract](/guides/work/dutch-employment-contract-explained-2026/).

## Who Is Covered by Dutch Social Security?

The starting principle: if you **work in the Netherlands** and your employer pays Dutch social security premiums (which they are required to do), you are covered by the Dutch system. Your eligibility for these benefits is tied to your employment status, not specifically to your permit type — though the type of work permit you hold can affect your options if you need to switch employers while on benefits. The [Netherlands work permit guide for non-EU nationals](/guides/legal/netherlands-work-permit-non-eu-2026/) covers the permit landscape, including what happens to your permit during periods of illness or job loss. Nationality and country of origin are not relevant. You could be on a work permit, a highly skilled migrant visa, or an EU freedom of movement basis — it does not matter for social security entitlement.

The exception is **posted workers** — employees sent from a company in another country to work temporarily in the Netherlands. Under EU Regulation 883/2004, posted workers can remain covered by their home country's social security for up to 24 months, using an A1 certificate. If your employer has arranged this, you are not building up Dutch benefits entitlement. Most expats hired directly by a Dutch employer are in the ordinary Dutch system.

**What premiums are being paid?** Look at your [loonstrook (payslip)](/guides/work/dutch-pay-slip-loonstrook-explained-2026/) and you will see deductions for volksverzekeringen (national insurance) and werknemersverzekeringen (employee insurance). The werknemersverzekeringen cover WIA, WW, and ZW — the benefits this guide covers.

## Ziektewet (ZW): The First Two Years of Illness

### How It Works

If you become ill and cannot work, Dutch law requires your employer to continue paying your salary for up to two years. This is called the Loondoorbetalingsverplichting bij ziekte — the obligation to continue wage payment during illness. The minimum is 70% of your last salary, but most collective labour agreements (CAO's) and individual contracts provide better terms — typically 100% in the first year and 70% in the second.

Your employer does not pay this directly from their own pocket indefinitely — they pay into a risk fund and/or take out Ziektewet insurance (WGA-ERD or WIA-eigenrisicodrager) to manage the liability. From your perspective, the payment simply comes in your regular salary.

### The Arbo Process

When you call in sick for more than a few days, your employer involves an **Arbo-dienst** — the Dutch occupational health service, which is a legally required component of Dutch employment. The Arbo-dienst's bedrijfsarts (company doctor) will assess your situation and put together a re-integration plan. This is not optional — the Dutch system is heavily focused on getting people back to work rather than simply paying them to stay home.

The key milestones in the two-year Ziektewet period:
- **6 weeks:** First assessment by bedrijfsarts, Plan van Aanpak (action plan) created
- **42 weeks:** UWV is notified of your long-term sick leave
- **1 year:** Progress evaluation; re-integration obligations become more formal
- **2 years:** WIA assessment begins (see below)

**Important:** If either you or your employer fails to meet re-integration obligations during the two years, UWV can extend the employer's payment obligation by up to a year as a sanction. This is called a loonsanctie. It is not common, but it is a real risk for employers who do not take the process seriously.

### Special Ziektewet Provisions

The ZW also covers specific groups where there is no employer to pay continuous wages:
- People on a temporary contract that ends during illness — UWV pays ZW directly
- Pregnant women during maternity leave (though this is the separate WAZO)
- Those whose employer has gone bankrupt

For expats on a **vaste dienstverband** (permanent contract), Ziektewet is largely invisible — your employer handles everything. For those on a **tijdelijk contract** (temporary contract), it is worth knowing that UWV acts as the payer if your contract expires while you are sick.

## WIA: Long-Term Disability After Two Years

After two years of continuous illness and the formal end of the employer's loondoorbetalingsverplichting, UWV conducts a **WIA-keuring** — a disability assessment. This involves two specialists:

1. **Verzekeringsarts** (insurance doctor): Assesses your medical condition, functional limitations, and what types of work are physically or mentally possible for you
2. **Arbeidsdeskundige** (labour expert): Based on the verzekeringsarts's findings, determines whether there are realistic jobs in the labour market you can still do, and if so, how many hours and at what pay level

The WIA is then split into two pathways depending on the outcome.

### WGA: Gedeeltelijke Arbeidsongeschiktheid (Partial Disability)

**WGA** (Regeling Werkhervatting Gedeeltelijk Arbeidsgeschikten) applies when you are assessed as partially disabled — meaning you have lost at least 35% of your earning capacity, but are still capable of some form of work.

**Who qualifies:** Anyone assessed as having lost 35-80% of their earning capacity, or 80-100% but where recovery is considered possible.

**What it pays:**

The WGA has two phases:

*Phase 1 — Loongerelateerde uitkering (wage-related benefit):* This pays 70% of the daily wage you are missing compared to what you could still earn. It lasts a minimum of 3 months and up to 38 months, depending on your work history. If you were earning €3,000/month and are now assessed as able to earn €1,000/month, you receive 70% of €2,000 = €1,400/month, on top of your €1,000 earnings.

*Phase 2 — Loonaanvulling or Vervolguitkering:* After the loongerelateerde phase, if you are actively working and earning at least 50% of your resterende verdiencapaciteit (remaining earning capacity), you receive a loonaanvulling — a supplement that tops up your earnings. If you are not meeting the earnings threshold, you receive the lower vervolguitkering, based on a percentage of the statutory minimum wage.

The maximum dagloon in 2026 on which benefits are calculated is **€282.50 per day** (approximately €73,450/year). If you earn above this, your benefit is still calculated on the maximum — you do not get more than the ceiling.

### IVA: Volledige en Duurzame Arbeidsongeschiktheid (Full and Permanent Disability)

**IVA** applies when you are assessed as fully disabled — unable to earn more than 20% of your previous wage — AND this is considered permanent (no realistic prospect of recovery).

**What it pays:** 75% of your dagloon, up to the maximum dagloon. This is the more generous of the two pathways. It is also a one-time assessment — if you qualify for IVA, you do not have to go through repeat re-integration obligations.

For an expat earning €4,000/month gross:
- Dagloon = approximately €185/day (annual salary / 261 working days)
- IVA at 75% = approximately €139/day = approximately €3,020/month gross

For someone earning at the maximum dagloon ceiling (€282.50/day):
- IVA = 75% × €282.50 = €211.88/day = approximately €4,600/month gross

### WIA Application Process

UWV contacts you around the 20th month of illness to begin the WIA process. You do not need to wait — if your illness is clearly long-term and serious, you can also request an early assessment. The application involves:

- Completing a **werkmap** (work dossier) documenting your re-integration efforts over the two years
- Medical documentation from your treating specialists and GP
- Your employer's re-integration verslag (report)
- The UWV assessment appointment (verzekeringsarts interview)
- A subsequent arbeidsdeskundige assessment

The full process from application to final decision typically takes 6-8 weeks. If you disagree with the outcome, you have 6 weeks to file a bezwaar (formal objection) and, if necessary, beroep (appeal to the court).

**Practical tip:** Get a copy of your complete employer's re-integration verslag before submitting. Gaps or poor documentation in this file can lead to delays or negative assessments.

## WW: Werkloosheidswet (Unemployment Benefit)

### Who Qualifies

WW is for people who lose their job involuntarily — through redundancy, end of contract, or dismissal (in some cases). You do not qualify if you resigned voluntarily, unless you can demonstrate that staying was untenable.

**The 26-week rule:** You must have worked in at least **26 of the 36 weeks** immediately before becoming unemployed. This is the primary qualifying criterion. Part-time work counts proportionally.

**The refusal rule:** If you are dismissed for serious reasons (dringende reden) — misconduct, fraud — you lose WW entitlement or it is delayed.

### How Long Does WW Last?

The duration depends on your work history:
- **First 10 years of work:** 1 month of WW per year worked
- **Years 11 onwards:** 0.5 months of WW per year worked
- **Maximum:** 24 months

So if you have worked for 5 years in the Netherlands, you qualify for 5 months of WW. If you have worked for 20 years (including abroad, in some EU treaty situations), you may qualify for up to the 24-month maximum.

### What Does WW Pay?

- **First 2 months:** 75% of your last daily wage (up to the maximum dagloon)
- **From month 3:** 70% of your last daily wage

For someone earning €3,500/month gross:
- Daily wage = approximately €162/day
- First 2 months: €122/day = approximately €2,640/month gross
- From month 3: €113/day = approximately €2,460/month gross

### Your Obligations While Receiving WW

WW is not passive income. You must:
- Register as unemployed with UWV within 1 week of your last working day
- Actively search for work and document your efforts
- Accept suitable work when offered (what counts as "suitable" broadens over time)
- Report any earnings immediately — you can work part-time and still receive partial WW
- Attend appointments with UWV's re-integration coaches

Failure to comply can result in a maatregel (penalty) — a temporary reduction or suspension of the benefit.

**How to apply:** Online via uwv.nl using your DigiD. If you do not yet have a DigiD, get one as soon as you are in the Netherlands — the [DigiD guide](/guides/legal/digid-guide-expats-2026/) explains the process.

## Bijstand: Social Assistance as a Last Resort

**Bijstand** (under the Participatiewet) is the safety net below everything else. It is means-tested, administered by your local municipality (gemeente), and designed for people who have no other income source and insufficient savings.

### What It Pays (2026)

| Household type | Monthly bijstand amount |
|----------------|------------------------|
| Single person (21+) | ~€1,175 |
| Couple (both 21+) | ~€1,678 |
| Single parent | ~€1,174-€1,341 |

These amounts are regularly adjusted and tied to the statutory minimum wage (WML).

### The Means Test

Before you qualify, UWV and/or the municipality will assess:
- All income (including partner's income)
- Savings and assets — the threshold is approximately €7,575 for a single person (2026). Assets above this must generally be depleted before bijstand kicks in
- Property — if you own a home, its equity affects eligibility

### Can Expats Claim Bijstand?

This is where residency status matters:

**EU/EEA citizens:**
- **First 3 months:** No right to bijstand (general freedom of movement period)
- **After 3 months as a worker or self-employed:** Generally entitled, same as Dutch nationals
- **After 5 years of legal residence:** Permanent residency right, full entitlement

**Non-EU expats on work permits:**
- Generally **not entitled** to bijstand — the work permit condition typically requires that you do not become a burden on public funds (geen beroep op de publieke middelen)
- An exception may apply if you have achieved permanent residency (after 5 years on a valid permit) — this is fact-specific and worth discussing with a social worker or lawyer

**The practical reality:** Most expats reading this guide will never need bijstand. But knowing where the floor is provides useful context when planning finances and understanding the Dutch social contract. ## WIA vs WW vs Bijstand: The Key Differences

| | **WIA** | **WW** | **Bijstand** |
|--|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Trigger** | Long-term illness/disability | Involuntary job loss | No income, no other benefit |
| **Based on** | Work history + disability assessment | Work history (26-wk rule) | Need (means-tested) |
| **Amount** | 70-75% of dagloon | 70-75% of dagloon | Fixed statutory minimum |
| **Duration** | Indefinite (while disabled) | 3-24 months | Indefinite (while qualified) |
| **Administered by** | UWV | UWV | Gemeente |
| **Obligations** | Re-integration cooperation | Active job search | Participation obligations |

## Documents You Will Need for Most Applications

Regardless of which benefit you are applying for, gather these in advance:

- **BSN** (Burgerservicenummer) — your citizen service number
- **DigiD** — for online applications
- **Valid ID** — passport or EU identity card
- **Employment contract** and most recent loonstroken (payslips)
- **Bank account details** (IBAN) — must be in your own name
- **Medical documentation** (for WIA/ZW claims)
- **Employer's re-integration verslag** (for WIA specifically)
- **Evidence of job search** (for WW — dates, names, outcomes)

## Getting Help

UWV has an **English-language helpline** — call 088 898 2000 and ask for an English-speaking agent. The quality varies, but for basic questions about eligibility and process it is useful.

For more complex situations — disputes about assessments, appeals, or situations involving posted worker status — a **rechtsbijstandsverzekering** (legal aid insurance) or a dedicated social security lawyer is worth the cost. Many Dutch law firms have specialists in UWV appeals, and the process can be won at the bezwaar (objection) stage without going to court.

The **Juridisch Loket** (juridischloket.nl) provides free first-line legal advice in the Netherlands. It has English-language options and can advise on your rights in social security disputes.

## The Bigger Picture: Social Security and Pension

Benefits like WIA and WW are distinct from your **pension** (AOW and second-pillar pensioen), though the same years of Dutch employment build up both. ## Practical Steps When You Become Ill or Disabled

If you experience illness or disability that affects your ability to work, the sequence of events in the Netherlands is quite specific. Here is what it looks like in practice:

**Week 1: Report sick to your employer.** Within the first week of illness, you must formally ziekmelden (report sick) to your employer. Many employers have an arbo (occupational health) service provider that you also notify. Do not delay this — the Wet verbetering poortwachter requires documentation from the first day.

**Week 6: First arbo visit.** Your employer's arbo service will schedule a conversation with a bedrijfsarts (company doctor) by week 6 at the latest. The bedrijfsarts is not your treating doctor — they assess your functional capacity for work, not your diagnosis. You are obligated to cooperate with this process.

**Week 8: Plan van aanpak.** If your illness continues, a plan van aanpak (return-to-work plan) must be in place by week 8. This is a joint plan between you and your employer, documented and signed, setting out re-integration goals and steps.

**Month 20: Eerstejaarsevaluatie.** After one year, a formal evaluation of your re-integration progress takes place. By this point, you should be in active re-integration, either with your current employer (spoor 1) or preparing for a different employer (spoor 2).

**Month 23: WIA application.** In the 23rd month of illness, you apply for a WIA benefit at the UWV. This triggers a formal assessment of your functional limitations and work capacity. The assessment determines whether you receive WGA or IVA, and at what rate.

**The re-integration obligation is mutual.** Your employer is obligated to offer suitable work (passende arbeid) and to fund re-integration activities. You are obligated to cooperate fully and make a genuine effort to return to work. Failure by either party has consequences — the UWV can impose sanctions on employers who do not comply, and benefits can be reduced for employees who refuse reasonable work.

## What to Do If Your WIA Application Is Rejected

If the UWV rejects your WIA application or rates your disability lower than you believe is correct, you have clear options:

**Bezwaar (objection):** Within 6 weeks of receiving the decision, submit a written bezwaar to the UWV explaining why you disagree. The UWV must review the decision and respond within 12 weeks. Getting a second medical opinion (second opinion bij bedrijfsarts) to support your bezwaar can strengthen your case.

**Beroep (appeal):** If the bezwaar is rejected, you can appeal to the administrative court (bestuursrechter) within 6 weeks. Legal aid (toevoeging via Raad voor Rechtsbijstand) may be available if your income is low.

**Legal representation:** The Juridisch Loket provides free initial legal advice. Many lawyers in the Netherlands specialise in UWV disputes and work on no-win-no-fee arrangements for social security cases.

Do not miss the 6-week deadlines — late bezwaar submissions are almost never accepted.

## Disability and Your Residence Permit

Being on WIA disability benefit does not automatically affect your residence permit if you are a highly skilled migrant or knowledge migrant. However, if your permit is linked to specific employment and that employment ends due to disability, your situation changes. Inform the IND promptly and seek specialist immigration legal advice — the interaction between disability, employment-linked permits, and Dutch social security law is complex and has case-specific outcomes.
