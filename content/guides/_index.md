Moving to the Netherlands is one of the most rewarding decisions you can make — but it comes with a steep learning curve. Dutch bureaucracy, the healthcare system, housing shortages, and tax rules can feel overwhelming when you first arrive. I have been through it myself, and I built these guides so you do not have to figure everything out alone.

This section brings together 38+ practical guides covering every major aspect of expat life in the Netherlands. Whether you are still planning your move or you landed last week, you will find step-by-step advice that is grounded in real experience — not government brochures.

## What You Will Find Here

The guides are organised into seven categories:

- **[Finance](/guides/finance/)** — Dutch taxes, the 30% ruling, banking, pensions, and sending money abroad. Financial decisions you make in your first year can save or cost you thousands of euros.
- **[Health](/guides/health/)** — How the Dutch healthcare system works, which zorgverzekering to choose, what eigen risico means, and when expat insurance makes more sense.
- **[Housing](/guides/housing/)** — Renting in a competitive market, understanding Dutch tenancy law, mortgages for non-residents, energy providers, and city-by-city cost of living breakdowns.
- **[Visa and Legal](/guides/legal/)** — The Highly Skilled Migrant visa, residence permits, DigiD registration, BSN number, and the full moving checklist.
- **[Work](/guides/work/)** — Working culture in the Netherlands, freelancing as a ZZP'er, coworking spaces, and career development as an expat.
- **[Integration](/guides/integration/)** — Learning Dutch, the inburgering exam, language apps compared, and building a life beyond the expat bubble.
- **[Daily Life](/guides/daily-life/)** — Internet providers, SIM cards, public transport, cycling, supermarkets, gyms, childcare, and the small things nobody tells you before you arrive.
