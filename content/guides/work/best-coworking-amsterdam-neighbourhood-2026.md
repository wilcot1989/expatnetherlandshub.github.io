I have worked from coworking spaces across Amsterdam for years — sometimes by choice (escaping apartment cabin fever), sometimes by necessity (client visits, team meetings, unreliable home internet). Amsterdam's coworking market has matured considerably since 2019. The pandemic-driven boom brought over-expansion, and the market has since consolidated, leaving behind a varied mix of quality operators.

The right coworking space depends heavily on what you actually need: proximity to clients, creative stimulus, quiet focus space, meeting rooms, or community events. Amsterdam's neighbourhoods each have a distinct character, and the coworking options reflect that.

Here is what I know about each. For coworking options outside Amsterdam, see my [best coworking spaces in the Netherlands guide](/guides/work/best-coworking-spaces-netherlands-2026/) and the [detailed coworking comparison](/guides/work/best-coworking-spaces-compared-netherlands-2026/).

## Centrum: The Business Core

Amsterdam's central district (Centrum) is not primarily a residential neighbourhood — it is the commercial and tourist heart of the city. Coworking here means excellent transport links, proximity to clients and hotels, and a professional atmosphere. It also means higher prices and the constant background noise of one of Europe's busiest tourist areas.

### WeWork Amsterdam

WeWork operates multiple locations in Amsterdam. Their Centrum offerings include:

**WeWork Westermarkt** (Keizersgracht 281)
One of WeWork's more atmospheric locations, in a canal house with original Dutch architecture modernised inside. More characterful than the typical WeWork aesthetic. Good for client meetings where the setting matters. Hot desk day pass: approximately €45-55.

**WeWork De Entree** (De Entree 99, Amsterdam Oost — technically on the Centrum/Oost border)
Larger facility near Amsterdam Arena. More corporate feel, bigger meeting rooms, good for teams. Monthly hot desk: approximately €350-450.

WeWork's app provides access to all global locations. For expats who travel frequently, a global membership is useful — work in Amsterdam one week, London the next.

### Spaces Amsterdam

Spaces is a Regus/IWG brand with multiple Amsterdam locations. Their style is deliberately creative and design-conscious — meant to feel like a "creative playground" rather than a corporate suite.

**Spaces Vijzelstraat** (Vijzelstraat 68)
Premium Centrum location in a heritage building. Great for client meetings. Day pass: approximately €45.

**Spaces Herengracht**
On the famous Herengracht canal. Smaller, more intimate. Favoured by consultants and creative professionals meeting clients.

Spaces memberships offer cross-location access across their global network.

### Mindspace Amsterdam

Mindspace operates a Centrum location (Herengracht) and another at Zuidas. Known for high-design interiors, good coffee, and a member community with regular networking events. Slightly more exclusive — membership is required for regular access, though day passes are available.

## Amsterdam Zuid: Fintech, Finance, and Scale-ups

Amsterdam Zuid — specifically the Zuidas business district — is home to most of the city's major law firms, financial institutions, consultancies, and international corporate offices. Coworking here skews corporate and professional.

### TQ (Technology Quarter)

TQ is Amsterdam's most prestigious tech-focused coworking and scale-up hub. Located at the Zuidas (Gustav Mahlerplein 2), it focuses specifically on scale-up companies in fintech, legal tech, and enterprise software.

Key features:
- Community of 150+ growth-stage companies
- Regular events focused on enterprise tech
- Access to investor and corporate partner network
- More exclusive than a standard coworking space — best for companies, not individual freelancers
- Monthly memberships from approximately €800

TQ is most valuable if you are building or scaling a B2B tech company and want proximity to the financial and legal sector clients at Zuidas.

### B.Amsterdam

Located on the former Elsevier publishing campus in Amsterdam West (close to the border with Sloterdijk), B.Amsterdam is one of the largest innovation campuses in the Netherlands. Approximately 700 companies work there, spanning deep tech, creative industry, and sustainable business.

The campus offers:
- Flexible coworking alongside company offices and lab spaces
- Event halls, podcast studios, photography studios
- Outdoor terrace and large common areas
- Community atmosphere with regular events
- Hot desk day pass: approximately €25-35
- Monthly memberships from approximately €200

B.Amsterdam is better value and more community-oriented than the Zuidas corporate spaces. If you want startup energy and diverse community without the Centrum premium, this is a strong option.

## Amsterdam Oost: Creative Tech and Media

Amsterdam Oost has developed rapidly as a neighbourhood and its coworking scene reflects its mix of creative, tech, and media businesses.

### A Lab Amsterdam

A Lab (Overhoeksplein 2, technically in Noord but immediately across the IJ from Oost) is worth mentioning here as it draws heavily from the Oost creative tech community.

Actually: A Lab is in Noord — see the Noord section below.

**For Oost proper:**

### Soho House Amsterdam (for day working)

Soho House (Spuistraat — actually Centrum, but drawing Eastern creative community) and the Eastern Hotel have workspace provisions popular with Amsterdam Oost's media and creative cluster, but these are member clubs rather than traditional coworking.

### Independent Spaces in Oost

Amsterdam Oost has several smaller independent coworking spaces that serve the neighbourhood's creative and freelance community:

**Open Worklab** (Rijnstraat area): A small, calm workspace popular with writers and designers. No-frills, focused on deep work.

**Lola Bikes & Coffee** (Linnaeusstraat): A hybrid bike shop and workspace — characterful, very Oost. Small but with a loyal community.

The vibe in Oost coworking is less corporate than Centrum and Zuidas — it suits creative professionals, independent consultants, and those who want a neighbourhood feel rather than a business district atmosphere.

## Amsterdam West: Creative Industry and Media

Amsterdam West — including Oud-West, Jordaan, and the area around Westerpark — has its own distinct creative and media cluster.

### Westergas Coworking

The Westergasfabriek (Pazzanistraat 33) is a former gas factory converted into a cultural and creative campus. Several coworking operations run from the site.

The character here is unlike Centrum or Zuidas — industrial buildings, large courtyards, cycling infrastructure, a strong community of film, media, design, and NGO organisations. Events, markets, and performances make the campus feel alive.

Hot desk day pass at Westergas operators: approximately €20-35. Monthly memberships from approximately €180.

### Ketelhuis / Filmhallen Area

Near the Filmhallen (a cultural cinema complex in a former tram depot), several small creative coworking spaces serve the media and film industry community. These are smaller, less polished than the big operators, but excellent for creative professionals who want to be around their industry peers.

### Seats2meet Amsterdam West

Seats2meet operates a concept where you can use workspace in exchange for sharing your professional knowledge — a time-credit model. They have a presence in various Amsterdam locations including West. It is a different model from standard coworking and suits professionals with expertise to share.

## Amsterdam Noord: The Creative Frontier

Amsterdam Noord was long considered peripheral — the neighbourhood across the IJ river that Amsterdam commuters avoided. That changed dramatically from around 2015 onward. The NDSM wharf has become one of the most genuinely interesting creative and coworking environments in the Netherlands.

### NDSM Wharf (NDSM-werf)

NDSM is a vast former shipyard on the north bank of the IJ. It is roughly 15 minutes by free ferry from Central Station — this is important context. You arrive by water, look out at Amsterdam across the river, and work in what feels like a different world.

The coworking offering at NDSM is distributed across several buildings and operators:

**Pllek NDSM**: A popular café-restaurant-event space with informal workspace. Not traditional coworking — more of a "third space" with WiFi and table space. Day use costs approximately €0 (café purchase) to €15-20 for reserved workspace.

**NDSM Loods**: The main hall has various tenant coworking and studio spaces. Prices vary by operator.

**Veem House for Performance**: Studio and workspace for performing arts professionals.

**Klokgebouw NDSM area**: Houses several independent creative businesses and small coworking operations.

The spirit of NDSM is different from all other Amsterdam coworking. It attracts:
- Artists and designers wanting studio-adjacent workspace
- Film and video production businesses
- Architecture firms
- Social enterprises
- Anyone who finds the corporate coworking aesthetic deadening

Monthly hot desk prices at NDSM operators: approximately €150-280, significantly below Centrum rates.

### A Lab Amsterdam (Overhoeksplein)

A Lab is situated directly across the IJ from Central Station, also in Noord. It is housed in a former Shell research building (Shell's Amsterdam HQ was in Noord for decades) and has a striking modernist architecture with excellent views over the IJ.

A Lab hosts approximately 250 companies and thousands of professionals. It is stronger on tech and creative than NDSM, and has a more structured coworking offering:

- Hot desk day pass: approximately €30-40
- Monthly memberships from approximately €225
- Meeting rooms, podcast facilities, event space
- Regular community events, talks, pitches

The community is genuinely mixed — tech, design, media, consultancy — and the location is increasingly connected as Noord has built out its transport links and restaurant scene.

## How to Choose

**For client meetings and professional impression**: WeWork Centrum, Spaces, or Mindspace. Canal-house settings work particularly well with international clients who expect Amsterdam atmosphere.

**For startup networking and investor access**: TQ (if you qualify) or B.Amsterdam. The communities here are genuinely valuable beyond just the desk space.

**For creative work requiring focus**: NDSM, Westergas, or smaller independent spaces in West or Oost.

**For the best value per euro**: B.Amsterdam and NDSM-area spaces. Both offer substantial community benefits at significantly lower prices than Centrum.

**For occasional use and flexibility**: WeWork's global app access or Spaces memberships offer the widest practical flexibility including travel use.

## Practical Tips

**Book ahead for popular spaces**: Major spaces like WeWork and Spaces show real-time availability in their apps. For smaller independents, call before showing up.

**Check the WiFi speed**: Coworking spaces vary enormously in upload/download speeds. If you run video calls or upload large files, ask about connection specs before committing.

**Evaluate the noise**: Open-plan coworking spaces in Amsterdam range from library-quiet to productively noisy. Visit during working hours, not on a tour with the sales team, to get a realistic sense of the ambient noise level.

**Trial memberships**: Most operators offer 1-week or 1-month trials. Use these before committing to a 6-month or annual contract.
