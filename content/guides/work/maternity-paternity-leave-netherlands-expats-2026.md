A client of mine — Priya, a software engineer from India who had been in Amsterdam for three years — found out she was pregnant and then spent the next two months trying to understand what she was entitled to. Her employer's HR department gave her partial information, she read conflicting things online, and she nearly missed the UWV application deadline because nobody told her the employer submits the request, not the employee directly.

Dutch parental leave is actually among the better systems in Europe once you understand it. But the terminology (zwangerschapsverlof, geboorteverlof, aanvullend geboorteverlof, ouderschapsverlof) is confusing even for Dutch people. Here is the complete breakdown for expats — what you get, what it pays, and exactly how to apply.

**The short version:** 16 weeks maternity leave at 100% UWV pay. Partners get 1 week at 100% + 5 weeks at 70%. Both parents get 9 weeks paid parental leave (70%) in the first year, plus 17 more weeks unpaid. ZZP workers have a separate scheme.

## Zwangerschapsverlof: Maternity Leave for Mothers

### How It Works

Dutch maternity leave is called zwangerschapsverlof (pregnancy leave) before birth and bevallingsverlof (delivery leave) after. Together they form a minimum of 16 weeks.

The split works like this:
- You must start leave **no later than 4 weeks before** your due date
- You can start as early as **6 weeks before** your due date
- After birth, you have a minimum of **10 weeks** of leave

The earliest you can start leave is 6 weeks before the due date. If you want to work longer into the pregnancy, you can, but you cannot start leave less than 4 weeks before your due date — Dutch law sets this as a floor to protect the mother's health.

### What Happens If the Baby Arrives Early

If your baby is born before the due date, any unused prenatal leave is automatically added to the 10-week postnatal period. So if you started leave 6 weeks before your due date and the baby arrived 3 weeks early, you would have used 3 weeks of prenatal leave — and the remaining 3 weeks get added to your postnatal leave, giving you 13 weeks after birth.

The total is always at least 16 weeks.

### What Happens If the Baby Needs Hospital Care After Birth

If your newborn requires hospitalisation and stays in hospital for more than 7 days after birth, the postnatal leave period can be extended. The extension equals the number of days the baby was hospitalised beyond 7 days, up to a maximum of 10 extra weeks. This requires documentation from the hospital.

### Pay During Maternity Leave

UWV pays 100% of your daily wage during maternity leave, up to the maximum daily wage of EUR 290.76 gross per day (2026).

If your salary is below this maximum, you receive your full salary. If your salary is above this maximum, you receive EUR 290.76 gross per day — some employers top up the difference, but this is not legally required. Check your [employment contract](/guides/work/dutch-employment-contract-explained-2026/) or CAO (collective labour agreement) to see if a top-up is standard in your sector.

### How to Apply

Your employer handles the UWV application on your behalf. Here is the process:

1. **Notify your employer in writing** at least 3 weeks before you want to start leave (most people do this around week 12-16 of pregnancy)
2. **Provide a due date statement** from your midwife (verloskundige) or GP (huisarts)
3. **Your employer submits the application** to UWV
4. **UWV approves and pays** directly to your employer, who passes it on with your salary

You do not apply to UWV yourself as an employee. This trips up a lot of expats who are used to applying for benefits directly.

## Geboorteverlof: Partner Leave (Birth Leave)

Since 2019, Dutch partners — fathers, co-mothers, or any other registered partner — have been entitled to geboorteverlof (birth leave) after the birth of a child.

### The 1-Week Entitlement (100% Pay)

Immediately after the birth, the partner is entitled to 1 week (5 working days) of fully paid leave. This is paid by the employer at 100% of salary, with no cap. You can take these 5 days within the first 4 weeks after the birth date — they do not have to be consecutive.

No application is required for this part. Notify your employer as soon as practicable and take the days. Your employer cannot refuse.

### The Additional 5 Weeks (70% Pay via UWV)

Beyond the initial 1 week, partners can take an additional 5 weeks of aanvullend geboorteverlof within the first 6 months after birth. This is paid at 70% of daily wage by UWV, up to the maximum daily wage.

You can take these 5 weeks:
- All at once (a continuous 5-week block)
- Split into shorter periods (minimum 1 week at a time, unless your employer agrees to smaller units)
- Any time within 6 months of the birth

**Important:** Your employer applies to UWV for this payment. You must notify your employer at least 4 weeks before you want to take the additional leave. UWV processes the application, and payment goes via the employer.

Some CAOs (sector-wide labour agreements) top up the 70% to 100%. This is common in the public sector and some large multinationals. Check your contract or ask HR.

### Who Counts as a "Partner"?

Dutch law defines the partner as the person registered as the child's other legal parent, or the person married to or in a registered partnership with the birth mother at the time of birth. Same-sex partners are fully included.

## Ouderschapsverlof: Parental Leave for Both Parents

Ouderschapsverlof is separate from and in addition to maternity/partner leave. Both the mother and partner are each entitled to 26 weeks of parental leave for each child, until the child turns 8.

### The Paid Portion (9 Weeks at 70%)

Since August 2022, the first 9 weeks of ouderschapsverlof are paid at 70% of daily wage (up to the UWV maximum) if taken in the first year of the child's life. This applies to both parents independently — so a two-parent household can access up to 18 weeks of paid parental leave between them, on top of the maternity and partner leave above.

The 9 paid weeks can be taken:
- Full-time (9 continuous weeks)
- Part-time (spread over a longer period, e.g., working 3 days per week instead of 5)

**You cannot carry over the paid 9 weeks past the child's first birthday.** After 12 months, any remaining paid weeks become unpaid.

### The Unpaid Portion (17 Weeks Until Age 8)

After the 9 paid weeks, each parent still has 17 weeks of unpaid parental leave available at any point until the child turns 8. This is useful for:
- Extended periods of childcare during the toddler years
- School transition periods
- Illness or caring needs

For unpaid leave, you keep your employment rights (your position is protected), but receive no pay. You also lose pension accrual during this period, though this varies by employer.

### How to Apply for Ouderschapsverlof

1. **Notify your employer in writing** at least 2 months before you want to start the leave
2. Specify the start date, duration, and whether you want full-time or part-time leave
3. **Your employer applies to UWV** for the paid weeks on your behalf
4. If your employer has objections to the timing (for operational reasons), they must raise this within 4 weeks — though they cannot simply refuse the leave

## ZZP and Self-Employed Parental Leave

Being self-employed does not mean you get nothing. It does mean the system works differently.

### ZEZ: Maternity Benefit for Self-Employed Mothers

Self-employed women (ZZP, freelancers, sole proprietors) are entitled to ZEZ (Zwangerschaps- en bevallingsuitkering voor zelfstandigen), which is UWV's maternity benefit for the self-employed.

**What it pays:** Approximately the minimum wage. In 2026, this is around EUR 1,934 gross per month (the exact amount is based on the statutory minimum daily wage at the time of your leave).

**How to apply:** You apply to UWV directly — unlike employed women, there is no employer to submit on your behalf. Apply at least 2 weeks before your intended start date via the UWV portal (uwv.nl). You will need to provide:
- Proof of self-employment (KvK registration, recent income tax assessment)
- Expected due date statement from your midwife or GP
- Your BSN

**Important:** ZZP workers who also have a partial employment contract alongside their self-employment are treated as employees for the employed portion and self-employed for the ZZP portion. The benefits are calculated separately.

### ZZP Partners

Self-employed partners have no statutory right to geboorteverlof or the paid 5 weeks of aanvullend geboorteverlof — these only apply when you are employed. Self-employed partners can, of course, simply not work during that period, but there is no UWV payment for it.

If you are ZZP and your partner is employed, your partner gets their full employed entitlements. Only the self-employed person loses the partner leave pay.

Some occupational disability insurance (AOV) policies include maternity coverage for ZZP workers — worth checking your policy.

## Adoption and Foster Leave

If you adopt a child or become a foster parent, Dutch law provides similar leave entitlements:
- **Adoptieverlof:** 6 weeks at 100% UWV pay, can be taken in the 26-week period around the adoption
- **Pleegzorgverlof:** Short-term paid leave when placing a foster child, similar to birth leave

Apply directly to UWV at least 3 weeks before the expected placement date.

## How the Netherlands Compares: UK, US, Germany

It helps to know where the Dutch system sits relative to where you may have come from.

| Country | Maternity Leave | Partner Leave | Parental Leave | Pay Level |
|---------|----------------|---------------|----------------|-----------|
| **Netherlands** | 16 weeks | 1 wk (100%) + 5 wk (70%) | 9 wk paid (70%) + 17 wk unpaid each | Good |
| **Germany** | 14 weeks | 2 months (14 months combined) | 12 months each (65%, max EUR 1,800/mo) | Strong for higher earners |
| **UK** | 52 weeks (39 paid) | 2 weeks (90% then flat rate) | Shared 50 weeks (limited pay) | Better duration, lower pay |
| **United States** | 12 weeks (FMLA, unpaid) | None (federal) | Varies by state/employer | Very limited |
| **Sweden** | Part of 480 days shared | 90 days reserved each parent | Up to 80% income-related | Best in class |

The Dutch system is strong on the paid partner leave (5 weeks at 70% is above average in Europe) and solid on maternity pay (100%). Germany offers more total leave for parents who want a longer period at home. The UK gives more total weeks but at lower rates after the first 6 weeks. The US federal system is by far the weakest among this group.

If you are moving to the Netherlands from the US particularly, the 16 weeks of fully paid maternity leave and the 6 weeks of partner entitlements represent a significant improvement over most American employers' offerings.

## Practical Timeline: What to Do and When

**At 12 weeks pregnant:**
- Inform your employer verbally (not legally required yet, but good practice)
- Check your employment contract and CAO for any additional entitlements above the legal minimum

**At 16-20 weeks:**
- Submit written notification to your employer stating your intended leave start date
- Your employer should request UWV confirmation — follow up if you do not receive this within 2 weeks

**At 26-32 weeks:**
- Confirm your exact leave start date in writing
- If self-employed: submit your ZEZ application to UWV now

**At birth:**
- Partner takes the 5 days of geboorteverlof immediately
- Within 4 weeks, notify employer of plan to take aanvullend geboorteverlof (the 5 weeks at 70%)

**At 3 months:**
- Begin planning ouderschapsverlof if you want to use the paid 9 weeks — remember this must be taken before the child turns 1
- Submit request to employer at least 2 months before intended start

## Your Rights During Parental Leave

During any form of maternity or parental leave, Dutch law protects your:
- **Employment contract:** Your employer cannot terminate your contract during pregnancy or maternity leave
- **Position:** You have the right to return to the same or equivalent position after leave
- **Pension accrual:** Continues during maternity leave and the paid weeks of partner and parental leave
- **Holiday entitlement:** Continues to accrue during maternity leave

One area expats sometimes misunderstand: **salary and holiday allowance.** The 8% vakantiegeld (holiday allowance) paid by Dutch employers annually continues to accrue during UWV-paid leave. Your employer still pays this on the full salary, even though UWV is covering the base pay. Check your [Dutch pay slip guide](/guides/work/dutch-pay-slip-loonstrook-explained-2026/) if you are unsure how this shows up.

## Benefits and Toeslagen During Parental Leave

Taking parental leave may affect your income and therefore your entitlement to government benefits (toeslagen). Specifically:
- **Zorgtoeslag** (healthcare benefit): Lower household income during parental leave may increase your entitlement. See the [Dutch health insurance guide](/guides/health/dutch-health-insurance-guide-expats-2026/) for how zorgtoeslag works.
- **Kinderopvangtoeslag** (childcare benefit): If you use registered childcare for other children while on leave, you can still claim this. See the [Dutch childcare guide](/guides/daily-life/dutch-childcare-expat-guide-2026/) for details.
- **Huurtoeslag** (rent benefit): Also income-dependent — temporary lower income during leave can affect your calculation. See the [zorgtoeslag and huurtoeslag guide](/guides/finance/zorgtoeslag-huurtoeslag-expats-2026/).

It is worth submitting updated income estimates to the Belastingdienst (tax authority) when you start any unpaid parental leave, so your toeslag payments adjust in real time rather than creating a large adjustment at year-end.

## Common Mistakes Expats Make With Dutch Parental Leave

**Assuming the employee applies to UWV:** For employed workers, you do not apply directly — your employer does. If your employer fails to submit, you may miss out. Confirm in writing that your employer has submitted the application and ask for the UWV reference number.

**Missing the paid ouderschapsverlof window:** The 9 paid weeks must be taken before the child turns 1. A surprising number of parents do not realise this and plan to take it later, only to discover it has become unpaid.

**Forgetting about CAO top-ups:** Many sectors (healthcare, government, education, large corporates) have CAO agreements that top up the 70% to 100%, or extend paid leave beyond the legal minimum. Always check your CAO or ask HR directly — never assume you only get the statutory minimum.

**ZZP workers not applying in time:** ZEZ applications need to go in at least 2 weeks before your start date. Given UWV processing times, I recommend applying at least 6 weeks before to avoid any gap in payments.

**Not considering the impact on your visa or residence permit:** If your residence permit is linked to employment and you take a long period of unpaid leave, check with the IND (Immigration and Naturalisation Service) about any implications. In most cases there are none, but it is worth confirming for specific permit types. ## My Advice: Do Not Leave Anything on the Table

Priya ended up getting everything she was entitled to — 16 weeks at 100%, her partner took the 5 days immediately plus the full 5 weeks at 70%, and they both took their paid 9 weeks of ouderschapsverlof in the first year. All told, between the two of them, they had over 7 months of paid leave combined.

The Dutch system is actually generous. The problem is nobody hands you a checklist. So here is mine:

1. Notify employer in writing at 16-20 weeks
2. Confirm employer submits UWV application — ask for reference number
3. Partner takes 5 days immediately, plans the 5-week block within 6 months
4. Both parents plan paid ouderschapsverlof before the child's first birthday
5. Check your CAO for any top-ups above the legal minimum
6. Update income estimates with Belastingdienst if taking unpaid leave
