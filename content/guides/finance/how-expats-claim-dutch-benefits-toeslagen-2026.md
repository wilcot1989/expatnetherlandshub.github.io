One of the most consistent things I see with expat clients: they have been living in the Netherlands for a year or two, paying for health insurance, rent, childcare — and nobody has told them they could have been receiving hundreds of euros per month in government allowances.

The Dutch toeslagen system is not well-advertised to international arrivals. It is buried in Dutch-language government websites. The amounts are significant — for some income levels, zorgtoeslag and huurtoeslag combined can provide €500-600/month in support. Kinderopvangtoeslag can cover most of your childcare costs entirely.

You paid Dutch taxes. These allowances exist for Dutch taxpayers. This guide is here to make sure you do not leave money on the table.

---

## What Are Toeslagen?

Toeslagen are income-dependent allowances paid by the Belastingdienst (Dutch tax authority). They exist to make essential costs — healthcare, housing, childcare, and raising children — affordable for lower and middle-income households.

There are four main toeslagen:

1. **Zorgtoeslag** — healthcare allowance (offsets health insurance premium)
2. **Huurtoeslag** — rent allowance (offsets rent costs)
3. **Kinderopvangtoeslag** — childcare allowance (offsets registered childcare costs)
4. **Kindgebonden budget** — child benefit top-up (extra support for families with children)

Each has its own income limit, its own rules, and its own application process. All are administered through the Belastingdienst and accessed via Mijn Toeslagen.

---

## Prerequisite: Getting a DigiD

To apply for any toeslag online, you need a **DigiD** — the Dutch digital identity used for all government online services. Without DigiD, you cannot access Mijn Toeslagen.

How to get DigiD:
1. Register at [digid.nl](https://www.digid.nl)
2. Enter your BSN (citizen service number)
3. Confirm your registered address — DigiD sends a postal letter to your registered address with a code
4. Activate with the code and your mobile number

The letter takes 5-7 working days. You must be registered in the BRP (population register) at your current Dutch address to receive it.

If you do not yet have a DigiD and believe you are owed past toeslagen, remember you can claim retroactively for the current year and the previous year (up to 1 September following the relevant tax year).

## 1. Zorgtoeslag (Healthcare Allowance)

### What It Pays

Zorgtoeslag offsets the cost of your Dutch basisverzekering (mandatory basic health insurance). In 2026, the estimated maximum amounts are:

| Situation | Maximum per month (2026) |
|---|---|
| Single person | ~€131 |
| Couple (two adults applying) | ~€257 |

*These figures are confirmed by the Belastingdienst annually; check the current year's amounts at toeslagen.nl.*

### Eligibility

You are eligible if:
- You are 18 or older
- You have Dutch basisverzekering
- You are a legal resident in the Netherlands
- Your annual assessment income (toetsingsinkomen) is within limits

**Income limits for 2026 (approximate):**
- Single: up to ~€38,520 (partial benefit below this; zero above it)
- Without partner: same threshold
- With toeslagpartner (partner living at the same address): combined income up to ~€48,224

At low incomes, you receive the maximum. As income rises, the benefit tapers. Calculate your exact amount at toeslagen.nl or using the government's toeslag calculator.

### Who Qualifies as Expat?

If you are registered in the Netherlands, have a BSN, have Dutch basic health insurance, and your income is within the threshold — you qualify. Nationality does not matter. Non-EU nationals with a valid residence permit qualify equally.

## 2. Huurtoeslag (Rent Allowance)

### What It Pays

Huurtoeslag helps with rental costs for lower-income households renting in the social or regulated private sector. In 2026:

| Situation | Maximum monthly huurtoeslag (2026) |
|---|---|
| Single person under 65 | ~€415 |
| Older or disability-specific rates | Higher — check current figures |

### Eligibility Conditions

**1. Rent ceiling**: Your monthly rent must be below the **liberalisatiegrens** (approximately €879/month in 2026). This is the threshold between social and liberalised (free market) rental housing. If your rent exceeds this, you do not qualify — regardless of income.

**2. Income ceiling**: Your toetsingsinkomen must be below approximately:
- Single: ~€31,340/year
- Multiple occupants (shared household): threshold varies

**3. Your own assets** must not exceed approximately €34,900 (singles) or €69,800 (couples). Savings and investments count here.

**4. The property must be your main residence** — registered in the BRP at that address.

### Important for Expats

The rent ceiling is the critical limitation. Most expats in Amsterdam, Utrecht, or The Hague rent at market rates well above €879/month, which means huurtoeslag is not available to them. However, if you live in:
- A smaller city (Enschede, Tilburg, Breda, Almere)
- Social housing (corporatiewoningen)
- A room with a modest rent

...your rent may fall below the ceiling and you may be eligible. Do not assume you don't qualify without checking.

## 3. Kinderopvangtoeslag (Childcare Allowance)

### What It Pays

This is the most financially significant toeslag for families with young children. Kinderopvangtoeslag covers a percentage of your registered childcare costs, up to a maximum hourly rate set by the government.

**2026 maximum hourly rates (government norms):**
- Dagopvang (full-day nursery, ages 0-4): ~€10.25/hour
- Buitenschoolse opvang/BSO (after-school care, ages 4-12): ~€9.12/hour
- Gastouderopvang (registered childminder): ~€7.53/hour

If your childcare provider charges more than these rates, you pay the difference yourself. Kinderopvangtoeslag covers only up to the government rate.

**Percentage covered by income level (2026, indicative):**

| Household income | Percentage covered |
|---|---|
| Up to ~€28,000 | 96% |
| ~€40,000 | 85-90% |
| ~€60,000 | 70-80% |
| ~€80,000 | 55-65% |
| ~€100,000 | 40-50% |
| Above ~€150,000 | ~33% minimum |

Even at high incomes, you receive some kinderopvangtoeslag. The minimum percentage is never zero for qualifying households.

### Eligibility

- You and your partner must **both work, study, or follow an inburgeringstraject** (integration course)
- The childcare provider must be **registered in the LRK** (Landelijk Register Kinderopvang — searchable at landelijkregisterkinderopvang.nl)
- Your child must be registered at your address
- One parent must be a **Dutch tax resident**

### The LRK Requirement

This trips up many expats. Informal childcare — an unregistered au pair, a friend who looks after your child, an overseas family member — does not qualify. The provider must be registered in the LRK. Most Dutch daycare centres, peuterspeelzalen, and BSO providers are registered; always verify before enrolling.

### How Much Can You Save?

Example: Family income €70,000, child in dagopvang 4 days/week (40 hours/month), government rate €10.25/hour. Monthly childcare cost: €410. At 65% coverage: kinderopvangtoeslag = ~€267/month.

Annual saving: ~€3,200. Over 4 years of preschool-age care: ~€12,800.

The numbers matter. Apply immediately when you register your child in daycare.

## 4. Kindgebonden Budget (Child Benefit Top-Up)

### What It Is

The kindgebonden budget is an additional monthly payment for households with children, on top of the base kinderbijslag (universal child benefit paid by SVB). It is income-dependent and tapers with higher income.

### 2026 Approximate Amounts

- First child: up to ~€155/month
- Each additional child: additional amounts
- Single parent supplement (alleenstaande ouder extra): up to ~€3,480/year additional

The kindgebonden budget is paid directly by the Belastingdienst and does not require separate registration from kinderopvangtoeslag — it is linked to your toeslag application and your registered children.

**Income limit**: Phases out from approximately €26,000 upward. At incomes above €90,000, the benefit may be zero for the child budget (though exact cutoffs change annually).

### Kinderbijslag vs Kindgebonden Budget

Note the distinction:
- **Kinderbijslag**: Universal child benefit paid quarterly by the SVB (Social Insurance Bank), regardless of income. Register for this separately through svb.nl. Amount in 2026: approximately €281/quarter for children 0-5, increasing by age.
- **Kindgebonden budget**: Income-dependent top-up, paid monthly by the Belastingdienst, only for lower-to-middle incomes.

Both are available to legal residents with children registered in the Netherlands.

## How to Apply: Step-by-Step via Mijn Toeslagen

### Step 1: Prepare

Before starting, have ready:
- Your DigiD (username and password)
- Your BSN and your partner's BSN
- Your income estimate for the current year (use your employment contract or last year's final tax return as a guide)
- Rental contract (for huurtoeslag)
- Childcare contract and provider LRK number (for kinderopvangtoeslag)

### Step 2: Log In to Mijn Toeslagen

Go to [toeslagen.nl](https://www.belastingdienst.nl/wps/wcm/connect/nl/toeslagen/toeslagen) and log in with DigiD.

### Step 3: Check Which Toeslagen Apply

Mijn Toeslagen shows you which allowances you are likely entitled to based on your registered circumstances. Use the built-in calculator to estimate amounts before applying.

### Step 4: Submit Application

Complete each toeslag application form online. You will input:
- Estimated annual income
- Household composition
- Rental details (for huurtoeslag)
- Childcare details (for kinderopvangtoeslag)

The Belastingdienst makes advance payments (voorschotten) based on your estimated income. These are adjusted at the end of the year when your actual tax return is filed.

### Step 5: Report Changes Immediately

If your income changes during the year, update it in Mijn Toeslagen immediately. Receiving too much toeslag based on an incorrect income estimate results in a terugvordering (repayment demand) the following year. This is one of the most common problems expats encounter.

Income changes to report: salary increase, bonus, change of employer, starting ZZP, moving in with a partner.

## Common Mistakes and Traps

### 1. Not Applying at All

The single biggest mistake. Nobody will proactively tell you to apply. Many expats only discover toeslagen after 2-3 years of being eligible. You can claim retroactively, but only back to 1 January of the current year (and the previous year if you apply before 1 September).

### 2. Underestimating Income

Claiming too much advance toeslag because you estimated income too low leads to repayment demands. When in doubt, estimate slightly higher.

### 3. Not Updating After Partner Moves In

If you have a toeslagpartner (someone registered at your address for toeslag purposes), their income counts toward your household assessment income. If a partner moves in and you do not update your application, you will likely receive too much toeslag and face a repayment demand.

### 4. Unregistered Childcare Provider

Kinderopvangtoeslag requires an LRK-registered provider. Always verify registration before you commit to a daycare arrangement.

### 5. Huurtoeslag With Too-High Rent

If your rent is above the liberalisatiegrens (€879 in 2026), you do not qualify. This is a hard cutoff, not a taper. Many expats in cities check and find they are just over the limit.
