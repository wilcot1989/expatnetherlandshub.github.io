## What Is Bunq?

Bunq is a Dutch digital bank, founded in Amsterdam in 2012. Unlike Revolut or N26, which are built outside the Netherlands and treat it as one market among many, bunq is a Dutch-born product. It holds a full European banking licence from De Nederlandsche Bank (DNB), which means your deposits are covered by the Dutch deposit guarantee scheme (DGS) up to EUR 100,000 — the same protection you get with ING or ABN AMRO.

The name "bunq" (lowercase, deliberately quirky) reflects the company's positioning: it wants to be the anti-bank, the bank that actually innovates rather than coasting on brand recognition. Whether you agree with that framing depends on how much you value features like tree planting and 25 sub-accounts versus walking into a branch and talking to a human.

For expats in the Netherlands, the most important thing to know about bunq is this: it has a real Dutch NL IBAN, it supports iDEAL, and it works exactly like a traditional Dutch bank for all practical purposes — minus the branches, and with a monthly fee starting at EUR 2.99.

## Bunq Plans: What You Actually Get

Bunq currently offers four paid plans. There is no free tier.

### Easy Bank — EUR 2.99/month

The entry plan. You get:
- One primary NL IBAN account
- One debit card (Mastercard)
- iDEAL payments
- Direct debits (SEPA)
- Basic savings feature
- The bunq app
- Up to 3 sub-accounts

This plan works for basic Dutch banking. If you just want a Dutch account for salary, rent, and day-to-day spending, Easy Bank does the job. The EUR 2.99 monthly fee is comparable to ING's basic account (EUR 2.45) and ABN AMRO (EUR 2.45).

### Easy Savings — EUR 2.99/month

Same price as Easy Bank, but focused on savings rather than spending. You get a savings account with bunq's current interest rate (competitive at around 3% in 2026 — check bunq's site for the live rate) but fewer spending features. Most expats who want bunq as a daily account should skip this one and go straight to Easy Bank or Easy Money.

### Easy Money — EUR 8.99/month

This is where bunq becomes genuinely interesting. Easy Money unlocks:
- **25 sub-accounts** — the feature bunq is most famous for
- Multiple cards (up to 3 physical and several virtual)
- Shared accounts
- Joint budgeting features
- Better cashback options
- Travel insurance (basic)
- Priority customer support

The 25 sub-accounts deserve their own section (see below), but for now: if you are going to pay for bunq, pay for Easy Money. The gap between Easy Bank and Easy Money in terms of actual functionality is significant.

### Easy Green — EUR 18.99/month

The premium tier. In addition to everything in Easy Money, you get:
- A metal card
- A tree planted for every EUR 100 spent (via bunq's reforestation partners)
- Carbon footprint tracking per transaction
- Extra international transfer options
- Premium customer support

Easy Green is bunq's lifestyle product. If you care about the sustainability angle and want the metal card aesthetic, it is a coherent offering. If you are primarily focused on practical banking at the lowest cost, it is hard to justify EUR 18.99/month when Easy Money at EUR 8.99 gives you almost everything.

## The 25 Sub-Accounts: Why This Matters

Most banks give you one account. Maybe two if you open a savings account separately.

Bunq Easy Money gives you 25 sub-accounts, all with their own IBAN, all visible in one app, all transferable between instantly.

Why does this matter for expats?

When you arrive in the Netherlands, your finances can get complicated fast. You might have rent arriving on the 1st of the month, health insurance debiting on the 5th, a savings goal for the next flight home, and spending money you need to keep separate from your buffer. With a traditional bank, you do this with spreadsheets and mental accounting. With bunq, you just make a sub-account called "rent buffer" and move the money there the day your salary lands.

My personal setup: I have sub-accounts for rent, utilities, health insurance, annual expenses (divided by 12 and moved monthly), holiday savings, emergency buffer, and day-to-day spending. My main account balance is always the money I can actually spend that week. This alone has made me calmer about money than I was with ING.

This system is not new — it is sometimes called the "envelope method" or "zero-based budgeting." Bunq just makes it simple to execute inside your bank rather than alongside it.

## iDEAL: The Feature That Makes Bunq Usable in the Netherlands

If you are new to the Netherlands, you will quickly discover that iDEAL is everywhere. It is the Dutch online payment system that links directly to your bank account. Bol.com uses it. Government portals use it. Most Dutch online shops default to it. Many landlords and utility companies expect it.

Revolut, for all its features, does not support iDEAL. N26 has limited iDEAL support. Many foreign bank accounts simply do not work with Dutch payment systems at all.

Bunq supports iDEAL fully on all plans. You pay online, your bank app opens, you confirm, the payment goes through in seconds. It works exactly as it does with ING or ABN AMRO. This is not a minor detail — it is the reason bunq functions as a real Dutch bank account rather than a digital add-on.

## Dutch NL IBAN: A Bigger Deal Than It Sounds

Bunq gives you a Dutch NL IBAN. Your account number starts with "NL" followed by two check digits and your bank details.

Revolut gives you a Lithuanian or Belgian IBAN. Wise gives you a Belgian IBAN. N26 gives you a German IBAN.

In practice, all European IBANs should be treated equally under EU law (SEPA). In practice, they often are not. Dutch employers frequently have payroll systems that flag non-NL IBANs and ask employees to provide a Dutch account. Dutch government payments (toeslagen, belastingdienst refunds) are paid more reliably to Dutch IBANs. Some Dutch landlords and subscription services also specifically require a Dutch IBAN.

Bunq's NL IBAN removes all of this friction. It is one of the clearest arguments for choosing bunq over Revolut if you plan to live and work in the Netherlands long-term.

## Savings: Competitive Interest Rates

Bunq's savings rate has historically been among the highest available from a Dutch bank. In early 2026, bunq was offering around 3% on Easy Savings balances — check bunq.com for the current rate, as this changes with ECB decisions.

For comparison: most traditional Dutch banks pay close to zero on standard savings accounts. ABN AMRO and ING have raised rates but still lag behind fintechs. Bunq's rate is competitive enough that some expats use it as their primary savings vehicle.

One important note: savings interest at bunq is not guaranteed and varies with market rates. Your deposits are covered by the DGS up to EUR 100,000.

I use the bunq app every day and have done for three years. Here is an honest account of the experience rather than a feature list.

**Notifications:** Bunq sends a push notification for every transaction, which is useful for tracking spending but can feel noisy if you are used to checking your bank weekly rather than constantly. You can adjust notification settings, but the default is very active.

**The interface:** The design is clean and opinionated. Bunq has a specific visual language — bright colours, large numbers, a card-first layout. Most users adapt to it quickly. If you have come from a conservative bank app, it feels different rather than better or worse.

**Sub-account management:** Moving money between sub-accounts is genuinely easy. You tap the sub-account, tap transfer, enter the amount. Five seconds. This is where the app earns its reputation — the feature is only as useful as the interface makes it, and bunq's interface makes sub-account budgeting easy enough that you will actually do it.

**Payment requests:** Bunq has a built-in payment request feature that lets you send a payment link via WhatsApp, email, or any messaging app. This is useful for splitting bills with other expats. It works even if the recipient does not have bunq — they click the link and pay by bank transfer or card.

**Card management:** You can freeze and unfreeze your card instantly from the app. You can create virtual cards for online shopping — useful if you want to use a separate card number for subscriptions so that a data breach does not affect your main account.

**Scheduled transfers:** You can set up automatic transfers from your main account to sub-accounts on a schedule. Combined with your salary payment date, this means you can automate your entire budgeting flow: salary arrives, money moves to rent, utilities, and savings sub-accounts automatically, and your spending account shows only what is genuinely available.

## Using Bunq for Dutch Government Payments

One area where bunq's Dutch NL IBAN matters directly: government-related payments.

**Toeslagen (benefits):** If you are eligible for huurtoeslag (rent benefit), zorgtoeslag (healthcare benefit), or kinderopvangtoeslag (childcare benefit), these are paid to your bank account by the Belastingdienst. Having a Dutch NL IBAN means these payments arrive without any complications.

**Belastingdienst refunds:** Tax refunds are paid by SEPA transfer to your registered bank account. An NL IBAN means this process works without any complications.

**DigiD and banking:** Some Dutch government digital services require verification via your bank account (iDIN system). Bunq supports iDIN, so you can verify your identity for Dutch government portals through your bunq account.

**UWV (unemployment benefits):** If you are ever in a situation where you need to deal with UWV for unemployment benefits, they process payments to standard Dutch bank accounts. Bunq works for this.

For expats navigating the Dutch financial system, the practical implication is that bunq functions identically to ING or ABN AMRO for all government-related transactions. This is not true for Revolut or foreign bank accounts.

## Bunq and the 30% Ruling

If you are eligible for the Dutch 30% ruling — the tax advantage for highly skilled migrants — your salary payments need to go to a bank account. Bunq's NL IBAN works for this. Your employer's payroll system will accept it without issue in virtually all cases.

Some expats ask whether they need a "proper" Dutch bank account for 30% ruling purposes. The answer is no — the ruling relates to your tax treatment, not your bank choice. Bunq, ING, Wise with a European IBAN, and ABN AMRO are all equally valid.

## How to Open a Bunq Account as an Expat

Opening bunq is entirely app-based. The process:

1. Download the bunq app (iOS or Android)
2. Choose your plan
3. Verify your identity with a passport or EU ID card
4. Enter your Dutch address and BSN

Bunq does not require an in-person appointment and does not have branches. Everything happens in the app. This is an advantage in terms of speed but means there is no fallback if you have a problem that requires human resolution.

## Bunq's Weaknesses: Where It Falls Short

Being honest about a product I use means acknowledging its limitations:

**1. Price**
EUR 8.99/month for Easy Money is notably more expensive than ING's EUR 2.45 or Rabobank's EUR 1.95. Over a year, that difference is EUR 77. The sub-accounts and features are genuinely good, but you are paying for them.

**2. Customer service**
This is the most common complaint I hear from expats using bunq. The in-app chat starts with an AI assistant. Getting to a human takes persistence. For routine questions (how do I do X?) this is fine. For anything involving a disputed transaction, a blocked account, or a technical error, the process is frustrating. There is no phone number. There are no branches. You are at the mercy of the chat queue.

**3. No physical presence**
Some banking tasks still benefit from a branch. Notarised bank statements, in-person identity verification for high-value transactions, and mortgage-related documentation are all smoother at a bank with physical locations. Bunq cannot help with any of this.

**4. Smaller product ecosystem**
Bunq does not offer mortgages, car loans, or investment accounts. If you are thinking about buying a house in the Netherlands, you will need a relationship with a traditional Dutch bank regardless of what you use for day-to-day banking.

**5. App-dependent**
If your phone is lost, stolen, or broken, accessing your account and replacing your card requires going through the app. Bunq's processes for account recovery are more cumbersome than a traditional bank with a branch you can walk into.

## Bunq and Long-Term Residency: What Changes After Year One

When you first arrive in the Netherlands, your banking setup is driven by necessity — you need an account quickly for salary, rent, and day-to-day expenses. By year one, the urgency is gone and you can make a more considered choice.

Most expats who initially took out whatever account was easiest (often ABN AMRO or ING, due to their established expat onboarding) reconsider their setup after the first year once they understand what they actually need. Here is what I see happen.

**The discovery of iDEAL.** Within the first few weeks, every expat discovers that Dutch digital payments run on iDEAL. Once you experience the gap (Revolut users especially) or the smooth experience (bunq and traditional bank users), you understand why the iDEAL-supporting account is the functional one.

**The sub-account lightbulb moment.** Many expats who have never used sub-accounts for budgeting try them at bunq and find it changes how they relate to their finances. Knowing that your rent money is sitting in a dedicated sub-account means you see clearly what is actually spendable. This is not a feature you know you want until you have it.

**The fee assessment.** After a year, you know what you actually use. If you have been using sub-accounts heavily, the EUR 8.99/month for Easy Money has demonstrably paid for itself in better financial management. If you opened Easy Money but only use one account, you are overpaying for features you do not use — downgrade to Easy Bank or switch.
