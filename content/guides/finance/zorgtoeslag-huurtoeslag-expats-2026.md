When I first moved to the Netherlands, I had no idea the Dutch government would effectively pay part of my health insurance premium and help with my rent. I assumed benefits were for Dutch citizens, not for someone like me who had arrived from abroad with a work permit. I was wrong — and that misunderstanding cost me several months of allowances I could have been receiving.

Zorgtoeslag (healthcare allowance) and huurtoeslag (rent allowance) are two of the most valuable financial benefits available in the Netherlands. Many expats qualify and never claim them. This guide explains how both work, who is eligible in 2026, how to apply, and — importantly — what can go wrong.

## What is Zorgtoeslag?

Zorgtoeslag is a monthly contribution from the Dutch government towards the cost of your health insurance. In the Netherlands, everyone is required to have basic health insurance (basisverzekering). The monthly premium for this insurance runs to roughly EUR 150-175 per month in 2026, which is a significant expense — especially for lower and middle-income earners.

The government recognises that this is a heavy burden, so it offers zorgtoeslag to offset part of the cost. The allowance is paid monthly, directly into your bank account, and it is calculated based on your taxable income from the previous year (or an estimate of your current-year income).

Zorgtoeslag is administered by the Belastingdienst (Dutch Tax Authority) through their toeslagen system — the same system used for all Dutch allowances.

For context on how health insurance itself works, see my [Dutch health insurance guide for expats](/guides/health/dutch-health-insurance-guide-expats-2026/).

## Zorgtoeslag Eligibility and Income Limits for 2026

To receive zorgtoeslag in 2026, you must meet all of the following conditions:

- You are 18 years or older
- You are registered in the Netherlands (ingeschreven in the BRP — see the [BSN registration guide](/guides/legal/bsn-registration-guide-2026/) if you have not registered yet)
- You hold Dutch basic health insurance (basisverzekering) with a Dutch insurer
- Your income is below the threshold (see below)
- You have a valid DigiD account (covered in my [DigiD guide for expats](/guides/legal/digid-guide-expats-2026/))

**Income limits for zorgtoeslag 2026:**

| Situation | Income limit |
|-----------|-------------|
| Single | approximately EUR 38,520 |
| With a toeslagpartner (fiscal partner) | approximately EUR 48,224 combined |

These figures are the *toetsingsinkomen* — your taxable income as assessed by the Belastingdienst. It is not your gross salary. Pension contributions, employer deductions, and the 30% ruling all affect this figure (more on that below).

The amount you receive decreases as your income rises. At lower incomes, zorgtoeslag can cover EUR 100-130 per month. At incomes near the threshold, it may be only a few euros. The Belastingdienst has a calculation tool on their website where you can check your expected amount.

**What does NOT qualify:**
- Students covered by a parent's policy (under 18)
- People with income above the threshold, even by one euro
- People whose partner's income pushes the combined total above the limit

## How to Apply for Zorgtoeslag

The application process is entirely online via the Belastingdienst's Mijn Toeslagen portal.

**Step-by-step:**

1. Log in to [mijn.toeslagen.nl](https://mijn.toeslagen.nl) with your DigiD credentials
2. Choose "Zorgtoeslag aanvragen" (apply for healthcare allowance)
3. Enter your estimated income for the current year — use your gross salary minus any known deductions, or consult your payslip
4. If you have a toeslagpartner (a partner registered at the same address with a fiscal link to you), include their income as well
5. Submit the application

That is it. The Belastingdienst will review and, if approved, start paying the allowance — usually within 2-3 weeks. The allowance is paid on or around the 20th of each month.

**Backdating:** If you forgot to apply, you can claim up to 12 months retroactively. So if you have been in the Netherlands for six months and only just found out about zorgtoeslag, you can claim from the month you first became eligible.

**Update your income estimate when things change.** This is critical. I will come back to this under "Common Mistakes."

## What is Huurtoeslag?

Huurtoeslag is a monthly rent allowance for people renting in the social or affordable private housing sector. Given how expensive Dutch rents have become — I have written extensively about this in the [cost of living in the Netherlands guide](/guides/housing/cost-of-living-netherlands-2026/) — huurtoeslag can make a genuine difference to monthly finances.

Like zorgtoeslag, huurtoeslag is administered by the Belastingdienst and paid monthly into your bank account.

Not everyone qualifies. The Dutch housing market has two main segments: social housing (huurwoningen with a rent below the liberalisatiegrens) and the private (free market) sector. Huurtoeslag is only available for social housing and some lower-rent private properties, not for high-end private rentals. If you are renting a flat for EUR 1,400 per month in Amsterdam, huurtoeslag is not for you. If you are in social housing paying EUR 700 per month, it may well be.

## Huurtoeslag Eligibility: Rent Cap, Income Limits, and Age

Huurtoeslag has more conditions than zorgtoeslag. You need to satisfy all of the following:

**Age:**
- You must be 18 years or older

**Rent ceiling (2026):**

| Age | Maximum qualifying rent |
|-----|------------------------|
| Under 23 | approximately EUR 454 per month |
| 23 or older | approximately EUR 880 per month (liberalisatiegrens) |

Your rent must be *at or below* these limits. Service costs and utility charges are generally excluded from the rent figure used in the calculation.

**Income limits (2026):**

| Household type | Approximate income limit |
|----------------|--------------------------|
| Single, under 23 | EUR 22,050 |
| Single, 23 or older | EUR 32,990 |
| Multiple adult household | EUR 32,990 (shared) |

Again, these are toetsingsinkomen figures — taxable income, not gross salary.

**Property requirements:**
- The property must be your main residence (hoofdverblijf)
- It must be a self-contained dwelling (not a room in a shared house without your own entrance and facilities)
- It must be registered at your address in the BRP

**Who is excluded:**
- Expats in temporary or serviced accommodation
- Students living in student housing managed by a housing corporation (separate rules apply)
- People whose rent exceeds the ceiling, even by one euro
- Short-stay or vacation rental situations

## How to Apply for Huurtoeslag

The application process mirrors zorgtoeslag — everything goes through Mijn Toeslagen.

**Step-by-step:**

1. Confirm your rent is below the applicable ceiling
2. Log in to [mijn.toeslagen.nl](https://mijn.toeslagen.nl) with your DigiD
3. Choose "Huurtoeslag aanvragen"
4. Enter your rent amount, your registered address, and your estimated income
5. If you have housemates who are fiscal partners or co-tenants, their income must also be declared
6. Submit and wait for confirmation

As with zorgtoeslag, you can backdate applications by up to 12 months. If you moved into your rental property six months ago and forgot to apply, do it now.

The allowance is recalculated annually when your tax return is processed. If your actual income differs from your estimate, you will receive either a top-up or a demand for repayment.

## Other Toeslagen Worth Knowing About

The Dutch toeslagen system includes two other allowances that may be relevant depending on your family situation:

**Kinderopvangtoeslag (childcare allowance)**
If you have children in registered childcare (daycare or out-of-school care) and both parents work or study, you may be entitled to kinderopvangtoeslag. In 2026, the government covers a significant portion of registered childcare costs — often 70-95% depending on income. Both parents must be working or in education to qualify. This allowance can be substantial and is well worth investigating if you have young children.

**Kindgebonden budget (child benefit top-up)**
This is an income-dependent supplement on top of the basic child benefit (kinderbijslag). It is paid automatically by the Sociale Verzekeringsbank (SVB) once you receive kinderbijslag, but you need to apply for the kindgebonden budget separately through the Belastingdienst if your income is low enough to qualify.

Both are administered through the same Mijn Toeslagen portal and share the same application logic as zorgtoeslag and huurtoeslag.

## How the 30% Ruling Affects Your Eligibility

This is where it gets interesting for many expats.

If you hold the [30% ruling](/guides/finance/30-percent-ruling-netherlands-2026/), your employer pays 30% of your gross salary as a tax-free reimbursement. This 30% is excluded from your taxable income. As a result, your toetsingsinkomen — the income used to calculate toeslagen — is based on 70% of your gross salary, not 100%.

**Practical example:**

Suppose you earn EUR 60,000 gross per year and hold the 30% ruling.

- Without the 30% ruling: taxable income = EUR 60,000 → likely above zorgtoeslag threshold, no allowance
- With the 30% ruling: taxable income = EUR 42,000 (70% of EUR 60,000) → depending on exact figure, possibly eligible for a small zorgtoeslag

The 30% ruling essentially shifts some expats into eligibility territory they would not otherwise be in. This is not a loophole — it is how the system works.

**Important caveat:** The 30% ruling also affects your pension accrual and other benefit calculations, so optimising for toeslagen alone is not a good strategy. Always look at the full picture. Use my [30% ruling calculator](/tools/30-percent-ruling-calculator/) to model your situation.

The rules around the 30% ruling changed again in 2024 and have been under ongoing legislative review — see the [30% ruling guide for 2026](/guides/finance/30-percent-ruling-netherlands-2026/) for current details.

## Common Mistakes — Including Painful Repayments

I see the same errors come up repeatedly with toeslagen. Most of them are avoidable.

**1. Not applying at all**
Many expats assume they do not qualify. They see toeslagen as "Dutch only" benefits or assume their salary is too high. Always check. The income thresholds are broader than people think, especially with the 30% ruling in play.

**2. The terugvordering problem — and how to avoid it**

This is the one that catches people most off guard, so it deserves a proper explanation.

When you apply for toeslagen, you enter an estimated income for the current year. The Belastingdienst pays your monthly allowance based on that estimate throughout the year. After the year ends, they reconcile the estimate against your actual taxable income from your tax return. If your actual income was higher than your estimate, they reclaim the difference. This is called a *terugvordering* (clawback), and it arrives as a formal repayment demand.

These repayments are not small. A repayment of EUR 500 to EUR 2,000 or more is common, particularly for expats who underestimated their income at the start of the year. Common triggers include:

- A mid-year salary increase or promotion
- A year-end bonus that was not accounted for in the estimate
- A partner moving in who earns income — their income is included in the combined toetsingsinkomen from the moment they register at your address
- A side income that you did not initially factor in

**What to do if you have already received a terugvordering letter:** Do not ignore it. The Belastingdienst expects payment within six weeks. However, if the amount is too large to pay in one go, you can request a payment plan (gespreide betaling) via Mijn Toeslagen. They will typically allow you to pay in monthly instalments, though interest may apply on deferred payments.

**The December deadline:** Each year, 1 December is the last date on which you can adjust your income estimate for the current calendar year and have it applied to your ongoing allowance payments. After 1 December, your estimate is locked for the year. Any underpayments will still be resolved at reconciliation, but you cannot reduce your monthly payments after that date. Mark it in your calendar.

**How to avoid a terugvordering:** The simplest rule is this — update your income estimate in Mijn Toeslagen the moment anything changes. A pay rise, a bonus confirmation, a partner starting work. It takes two minutes to adjust, and updating early will reduce or eliminate the year-end repayment demand entirely. If you are unsure whether a particular income change affects your estimate, err on the side of updating upward. Overpaying slightly throughout the year is far less painful than a large lump-sum demand in spring.

**3. Not reporting a toeslagpartner**
If you are registered at the same address as a partner, the Belastingdienst may automatically link your incomes as a "toeslagpartner." If you do not declare this correctly, you risk miscalculating your entitlement — and being asked to pay back a significant amount.

**4. Continuing to receive allowances after circumstances change**
If your income increases above the threshold, you move to a more expensive rental, your partner starts earning, or you leave the Netherlands, you must notify the Belastingdienst. Failing to do so and continuing to receive allowances is not just a mistake — it can result in formal recovery actions.

**5. Missing the deadline for filing your tax return**
Your toeslag is reconciled against your annual tax return (aangifte). If you do not file on time, the reconciliation is delayed, and in some cases the Belastingdienst makes a provisional assessment that may not be in your favour. See the [guide to filing your Dutch tax return](/guides/finance/dutch-tax-return-how-to-file-2026/) for details on deadlines and the filing process.

## Timeline and Payment Schedule

Understanding when things happen helps you plan ahead.

**Application:**
- You can apply at any time during the calendar year
- Allowances are granted from the first day of the month you apply (not the first day of the year, unless you apply in January or backdate)
- Backdating is possible up to 12 months

**Monthly payments:**
- Zorgtoeslag and huurtoeslag are paid on approximately the 20th of each month
- Payments land directly in the bank account you register with the Belastingdienst

**Annual reconciliation:**
- After the calendar year ends, the Belastingdienst reconciles your estimated income against your actual taxable income (from your tax return)
- This reconciliation typically happens between April and July of the following year
- You receive either a top-up payment or a repayment demand

**Provisional continuation:**
- If you do not actively stop your toeslagen, they continue into the next year automatically based on your last known income — but the estimate may need updating

**Key dates for 2026:**
- Tax year 2025 returns are due by 1 May 2026 (extensions available via a tax advisor)
- Toeslag reconciliation for 2025 typically completes by mid-2026
- You can file for 2026 toeslagen at any time during 2026

## The First Month: What I Wish Someone Had Told Me

When I moved to the Netherlands, toeslagen were not on my radar at all. I was focused on finding a flat, setting up a bank account, and surviving the first week on a borrowed bike without getting hit by a tram. Toeslagen were something I discovered nine months in, when a Dutch colleague mentioned the zorgtoeslag almost offhand.

That conversation was worth about EUR 1,100 to me — the amount I could retroactively claim for the months I had missed. I still feel slightly annoyed about those nine months.

The system does not announce itself. There is no welcome letter from the government saying "by the way, you may now be entitled to monthly payments." You have to know it exists, know you qualify, and proactively apply. This is not intuitive for people moving from countries with automatic benefit systems.

### What to Do in Your First Week

1. Register at the gemeente and get your BSN — this takes 1-3 weeks to arrive by post after you register.
2. Open a Dutch bank account — you need a BSN for most accounts, so this follows BSN registration.
3. Take out Dutch basisverzekering — required for zorgtoeslag eligibility. Compare on Independer and choose based on premium and eigen risico.
4. Apply for DigiD if you haven't already — required for Mijn Toeslagen access.
5. Once DigiD is active and you have your BSN, log in to mijn.toeslagen.nl and apply for whatever you qualify for.

Total time for step 5: about 20-30 minutes. Total potential annual value: EUR 1,000-3,000+ depending on your situation. Few tasks in your first month have a better time-to-money ratio.

### A Note on Household Registration

One thing that affects toeslagen more than people realise is your household composition as registered in the BRP. If you share a house with friends or housemates who are also registered at the address, they are generally not treated as toeslagpartners — only people who are your registered partner, or who have a fiscal link to you, count.

However, if a romantic partner moves in and registers at your address, they become your toeslagpartner immediately, and their income counts toward your combined toetsingsinkomen. This can push you over the income threshold for certain toeslagen. Update your application in Mijn Toeslagen the moment someone registers at your address and you will avoid a large repayment demand at year-end.
