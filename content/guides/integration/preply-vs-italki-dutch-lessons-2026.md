When I first moved to the Netherlands, my Dutch was, charitably speaking, terrible. I had grown up with a Dutch mother who largely refused to speak Dutch at home — "You need proper English," she would say, as if our Midlands town were under threat of a Dutch linguistic invasion. So I arrived in Amsterdam with roughly fifteen words, all of them related to food.

I signed up for a group language course at a local school, which helped, but I craved something more flexible and more personalised. A friend suggested online tutoring, and I spent the next several months testing both Preply and iTalki. I went through six different Dutch tutors across the two platforms, tracked my progress, and — because apparently I cannot do anything without turning it into a project — took notes on the whole experience.

Here is what I found.

## What Is Preply?

Preply is an online tutoring marketplace founded in 2012 and now one of the larger platforms of its kind globally. It connects learners with language tutors for one-to-one video lessons, and it has put significant effort into building a contained learning environment rather than simply connecting you with someone on Zoom and leaving you to it.

When you sign up, Preply walks you through a short questionnaire about your current Dutch level, your goals (survival Dutch, professional communication, exam preparation, conversational fluency), and your scheduling preferences. It then suggests a list of tutors who match your needs. Each tutor profile includes a video introduction, qualifications, reviews from past students, and pricing.

Preply vets its tutors before they can list on the platform. This does not mean every tutor is outstanding, but it does mean you are unlikely to book a lesson and discover the person has no idea how to teach. The platform also has built-in lesson tools — a shared digital whiteboard, file sharing, and the ability to set lesson goals and track your vocabulary directly in the interface. Your lesson history, homework, and word lists are all stored in your account.

---

## What Is iTalki?

iTalki launched in 2007 and is arguably the original marketplace for one-to-one online language tutoring. It takes a more open approach than Preply: anyone can list as a "community tutor" (essentially a native speaker offering informal conversation practice), while "professional teachers" must demonstrate teaching qualifications or experience.

This distinction matters for Dutch learners. On iTalki, you can choose from:

- **Professional teachers**: Qualified language teachers with formal credentials, typically EUR 15-30 per hour for Dutch
- **Community tutors**: Native or near-native Dutch speakers offering informal practice and conversation, typically EUR 8-20 per hour

The flexibility is one of iTalki's biggest selling points. Lessons happen on your preferred video platform — usually Zoom, Skype, or Google Meet. There is no contained platform environment, which means the lesson experience depends almost entirely on the individual tutor. A brilliant tutor brings structure, materials, and methodology. A less experienced community tutor might simply chat with you for an hour, which can be valuable but is rarely enough on its own.

## Pricing in Detail

### Preply Pricing

On Preply, Dutch tutor rates in 2026 typically run from EUR 15 for newer tutors to EUR 40 or more for highly experienced teachers with strong reviews. The majority of well-reviewed Dutch tutors sit in the EUR 20-30 per hour range.

Preply sells lesson credits in packages rather than charging per individual lesson. You buy credits upfront and use them to book sessions with your chosen tutor. If your tutor is unavailable or you want to change, unused credits can be applied to a different tutor.

The trial lesson is priced at a rate set by each tutor, but typically around 50% of their normal hourly rate. If you are unhappy with your first trial lesson, Preply will give you credits to try another tutor for free — a proper satisfaction guarantee that I found genuinely reassuring when I first signed up.

### iTalki Pricing

iTalki charges per lesson, though many tutors offer packages if you book multiple sessions upfront. Professional Dutch teachers charge EUR 15-30 per hour; community tutors charge EUR 8-20. A skilled community tutor at EUR 12 per hour with a structured approach can offer remarkable value.

Trial lessons on iTalki are listed separately by each tutor — typically a 30-minute session for EUR 5-15. Not every tutor offers a trial, so check the profile before booking.

One thing to note: iTalki adds a small service fee on top of tutor rates when you purchase credits. It is not large, but worth factoring in.

### Cost Over Three Months

To make this concrete: if you study twice a week for three months (roughly 24 lessons), here is what you might pay:

| Scenario | Platform | Cost Estimate |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| Experienced professional tutor, 1hr/session | Preply | EUR 480-720 |

| Beginner tutor (newer, lower rates), 1hr/session | Preply | EUR 360-480 |

The cheapest credible option is a good community tutor on iTalki. The most predictably quality-controlled option is a mid-range professional tutor on Preply.

## Tutor Selection and Vetting

### How Preply Vets Tutors

Preply requires tutors to submit a profile with a video introduction, and each application goes through an approval process. The platform checks that tutors meet basic quality criteria before they can go live. Tutors also build up reviews over time, and Preply surfaces highly-rated tutors prominently.

The Dutch tutor pool on Preply is smaller than iTalki's — roughly 100-200 active profiles at any given time. This sounds like a limitation, but in practice it means the selection is more curated. I found it easier to identify genuinely strong tutors on Preply because the pool of mediocre ones was thinner.

### How iTalki Vets Tutors

iTalki applies different standards to its two tutor categories. Professional teachers must submit evidence of qualifications or teaching experience, and their profiles are reviewed before approval. Community tutors go through a lighter review process — they can teach as long as they are a native or near-native speaker, but formal teaching credentials are not required.

This means more variability. On iTalki, I had one of the best Dutch lessons I have ever experienced — a former secondary school teacher offering sessions at EUR 22 per hour who had clearly thought hard about how adults learn a new language. I also had a community tutor session that was essentially an extended Dutch conversation with a lovely person who had no framework for helping me improve systematically. Both are legitimate uses of the platform, but you need to know what you are looking for.

**The practical tip:** On iTalki, filter specifically for professional teachers and read reviews carefully. Look for reviewers who mention similar goals to yours.

## Lesson Structure and Learning Experience

### Preply: More Structure Built In

Preply's platform is built around the idea of ongoing tutor-student relationships with measurable progress. When you book with a tutor, you can set specific learning goals in your profile. Tutors can assign homework, share files, and track vocabulary you are learning. Between lessons, you can review notes and materials in your Preply account.

The integrated video system — called Preply Space — keeps everything in one place. It includes a shared whiteboard for writing out grammar examples, and a note-taking area. It is not technically superior to Zoom, but it means your lesson history and materials stay organised without you having to do anything.

This structure suits learners who want accountability and do not particularly want to manage their own learning resources.

### iTalki: More Flexibility, More Responsibility

On iTalki, the lesson experience is entirely shaped by your tutor and your own preferences. A professional teacher will typically conduct lessons in a similar way to Preply — structured curriculum, homework, materials — but delivered via Zoom or Skype and managed through Google Docs or their own resources. A community tutor might simply talk with you in Dutch, correct errors as they occur, and let you guide the topic.

The lack of a built-in platform means your lesson history and materials are scattered across email threads, shared documents, and video call recordings. It requires you to be more organised and proactive about your own learning.

For self-directed learners, this freedom is welcome. For beginners who are not yet sure what to ask for, it can feel unstructured.

## Scheduling and Cancellation

Both platforms allow you to browse tutor availability and book specific time slots.

**Preply** requires 4 hours notice for lesson cancellation. Cancel within 4 hours and you forfeit the lesson credit. This is stricter than many learners expect, so set calendar reminders.

**iTalki** cancellation policies vary by tutor, but the standard is 24 hours notice. Some tutors allow shorter notice; others are stricter. Check each tutor's policy before you book.

In terms of availability, both platforms have Dutch tutors across multiple time zones, which helps if you work irregular hours. However, the most popular and highly-reviewed tutors on both platforms tend to fill up quickly — especially in the evening slots that working expats prefer. Book ahead.

---

## Video Quality and Technical Experience

Preply's integrated video tool is functional and reliable. It works directly in the browser without requiring any additional software. Screen sharing, whiteboard, and chat are all available within the lesson interface. For most learners it is perfectly adequate.

iTalki relies on whatever platform you and your tutor agree to use. Zoom is the most common choice, and Zoom's video and audio quality is genuinely excellent. Some learners actually prefer this arrangement because Zoom offers better video quality and more stable performance than browser-based solutions.

If you already use Zoom regularly for work, iTalki's approach will feel natural. If you prefer a simpler, all-in-one setup, Preply wins.

## Progress Tracking and Learning Materials

This is one of Preply's clearer advantages. The platform includes:

- A built-in vocabulary tool where tutors and students can log new words
- Lesson notes that persist in your account
- Goal tracking visible to both you and your tutor
- A dashboard showing your lesson history and hours studied

iTalki has no equivalent built-in tracking. Progress monitoring is entirely up to you and your tutor to manage externally. For self-organised learners this is fine; for everyone else, it requires more effort.

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of Online Dutch Tutoring

Whether you choose Preply or iTalki, these practices make a significant difference:

**1. Try at least two tutors before settling.** Teaching chemistry is personal. The best Dutch teacher in the world might not be the right match for your learning style. Use trial lessons on both platforms to find someone whose approach clicks with you.

**2. Come with clear goals.** "I want to get better at Dutch" is not a goal; "I want to be able to hold a full conversation at my next company borrel" is. Share specific objectives with your tutor so they can structure lessons accordingly.

**3. Study between sessions.** One hour of tutoring per week is not enough on its own. Use apps like Babbel or Duolingo to study daily between lessons — see our [Babbel vs Duolingo comparison](/guides/integration/babbel-vs-duolingo-learn-dutch-2026/) for which suits your style. Your tutor time becomes dramatically more valuable when you are reinforcing learning independently.

**4. Ask for homework.** Good tutors will assign it anyway, but if yours does not, ask explicitly. Even ten minutes of targeted exercises between sessions compounds over months.

**5. Record yourself.** Many learners are surprised by how their Dutch sounds compared to how they imagine it sounds. Record practice sentences between lessons and share them with your tutor for feedback.

**6. Be consistent.** Two lessons per week for three months will get you further than six lessons per week for three weeks and then a month off. Regularity matters more than intensity.

## Who Should Choose Preply?

Preply is the better fit if you:

- Are a complete beginner who wants guidance on what to study and in what order
- Want a vetted tutor with clear credentials and strong reviews
- Prefer having progress tracking and lesson materials managed in one place
- Value a platform satisfaction guarantee to reduce the risk of a bad tutor experience
- Are willing to pay a modest premium for a more contained, managed learning experience
- Want consistency with the same tutor week after week
- Find self-organisation difficult and benefit from built-in accountability tools

---

## Who Should Choose iTalki?

iTalki is the better fit if you:

- Are at an intermediate level and know what kind of lesson you want
- Want access to a wider pool of tutors, including informal native speakers at lower rates
- Prefer the flexibility to use your own video platform and manage your own materials
- Are on a tighter budget and want to explore community tutors alongside professional options
- Want more choice in tutor style, background, and approach
- Are comfortable reading through many profiles and making your own assessment of quality

---

## The Case for Using Both

There is nothing stopping you from booking a professional teacher on Preply for structured weekly lessons and occasionally booking a community tutor on iTalki for cheap, informal conversation practice in between. The two platforms serve slightly different purposes, and using them together — structured learning plus conversation immersion — can be more effective than either alone.

Pair this with a self-study app and you have a three-track approach that covers grammar instruction, speaking practice, and daily vocabulary reinforcement. It is the method I recommend to most expat clients who are serious about reaching conversational Dutch within a year.

---

## Final Verdict

Both Preply and iTalki are legitimate, well-run platforms with genuine Dutch tutors and real success stories. The right choice depends on your situation:

**Choose Preply** if you are a beginner, value a managed learning experience, and want the reassurance of platform-level quality control and a satisfaction guarantee. The slightly higher prices are justified by the structure, vetting, and built-in tools.

**Choose iTalki** if you are at an intermediate level, want more tutor choice at a broader price range, and are comfortable taking a more active role in managing your own learning. The community tutor option offers outstanding value if you find the right person.

My personal verdict after testing both: I got more out of my Preply sessions as a beginner because the structure kept me on track. Once my Dutch reached a functional conversational level, I found more value in iTalki's diverse tutor pool for practising different contexts — work conversations, informal chats, formal writing. The platforms suit different stages of the learning journey, which is why so many serious Dutch learners end up using both at some point.

Whatever platform you choose, the best thing you can do is book a trial lesson this week rather than spending another fortnight reading comparisons. The tutors are ready; the only question is whether you are.
