Choosing where to live in the Netherlands is one of the biggest decisions you will make as an expat. Each city has a different character, cost of living, and job market. I have lived in or spent significant time in all seventeen cities below, and these guides reflect what I actually experienced — not what the tourist board wants you to think.

Use the [Housing Budget Checker](/tools/housing-budget-checker/) to see what you can afford in each city before you start looking.

## City Guides

- [Amsterdam](/cities/amsterdam/) — largest international community, highest rents, best job market
- [Rotterdam](/cities/rotterdam/) — underrated, affordable, growing expat scene
- [The Hague](/cities/the-hague/) — government, diplomacy, NGOs, international law
- [Utrecht](/cities/utrecht/) — central location, families, strong quality of life
- [Eindhoven](/cities/eindhoven/) — tech and engineering hub (ASML, Philips, NXP)
- [Leiden](/cities/leiden/) — academic, life sciences, between Amsterdam and The Hague
- [Groningen](/cities/groningen/) — most affordable, student city, far north
- [Maastricht](/cities/maastricht/) — southern character, EU institutions, lowest rents
- [Haarlem](/cities/haarlem/) — 20 minutes from Amsterdam, historic centre, quieter pace
- [Delft](/cities/delft/) — TU Delft, engineering, near The Hague
- [Almere](/cities/almere/) — planned city, 25 minutes from Amsterdam, affordable rents
- [Amstelveen](/cities/amstelveen/) — south of Amsterdam, large international community, Schiphol proximity
- [Tilburg](/cities/tilburg/) — North Brabant, logistics, Tilburg University, low rents
- [Arnhem](/cities/arnhem/) — Gelderland capital, energy sector, near Veluwe nature area
- [Nijmegen](/cities/nijmegen/) — oldest Dutch city, Radboud University, NXP Semiconductors
- [Breda](/cities/breda/) — near Belgian border, growing professional services sector
- [Den Bosch](/cities/den-bosch/) — mid-sized, well-connected, good for families
