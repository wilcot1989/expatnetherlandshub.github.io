<h2>What is the 30% ruling?</h2>
<p>
The 30% ruling (<em>30%-regeling</em> in Dutch) is a favourable tax facility offered by the Dutch Tax Authority (<em>Belastingdienst</em>) to highly skilled migrants recruited from abroad. When your employer pays you a tax-free allowance of up to 30% of your salary to compensate for the extra costs of relocating to and living in the Netherlands — known as "extraterritorial costs" — you end up paying income tax on a significantly smaller portion of your gross salary.
</p>
<p>
In practical terms: if you earn €80.000 gross per year and qualify for the 30% ruling in the first phase, only €56.000 (70%) is subject to Dutch income tax. The remaining €24.000 is treated as a tax-free reimbursement. This can result in thousands of euros in annual tax savings.
</p>

<div class="callout success">
<strong>Key benefit at a glance</strong>
On a salary of €80.000, the 30% ruling saves approximately €8.000–€10.000 in income tax per year in the first phase. Use the calculator above for your personal figure.
</div>

<h2>Who qualifies for the 30% ruling in 2026?</h2>
<p>To benefit from the 30% ruling in 2026, you must meet all of the following criteria:</p>
<ul>
<li>You are employed by a Dutch employer (or a Dutch permanent establishment of a foreign employer).</li>
<li>You were recruited from abroad — meaning you lived outside the Netherlands before starting your Dutch job.</li>
<li>You lived more than 150 km from the Dutch border for at least 16 of the 24 months before your first working day in the Netherlands.</li>
<li>You possess specific expertise that is scarce in the Dutch labour market.</li>
<li>Your taxable salary (after applying the ruling) meets the minimum threshold.</li>
<li>Both you and your employer have applied for the ruling together, and it has been granted by the Belastingdienst.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Minimum salary threshold 2026</h3>
<p>The taxable salary (i.e. the salary <em>after</em> deducting the 30% tax-free allowance) must be at least:</p>
<table class="info-table" aria-label="Minimum salary thresholds for 30% ruling 2026">
<thead>
<tr>
<th>Category</th>
<th>Minimum taxable salary</th>
<th>Approximate required gross salary</th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td>Standard (30 years or older)</td>
<td>€46.107</td>
<td>approx. €65.867</td>
</tr>
<tr>

<td>€35.048</td>
<td>approx. €50.069</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p>
These thresholds are updated annually. Note that the <em>gross</em> salary required is higher than the taxable minimum because the ruling itself reduces the taxable base.
</p>

<h2>The step-down rule explained (2024–2026)</h2>
<p>
Since 1 January 2024, the Dutch government introduced a step-down schedule for new 30% ruling applications. Instead of receiving 30% tax-free for the full five years, the percentage decreases in three phases:
</p>

<table class="info-table" aria-label="30% ruling step-down percentages from 2024">
<thead>
<tr>
<th>Phase</th>
<th>Duration</th>
<th>Tax-free percentage</th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td>Phase 1</td>
<td>Months 1–20 (approx. years 1–2)</td>
<td>30%</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Phase 2</td>
<td>Months 21–40 (approx. years 2–4)</td>
<td>20%</td>
</tr>
<tr>

<td>Months 41–60 (approx. years 4–5)</td>
<td>10%</td>
</tr>
</tbody>

<div class="callout warning">
<strong>Transitional arrangement for ruling holders before 2024</strong>
If you already had an approved 30% ruling before 1 January 2024, you are not affected by the step-down. You continue to receive the full 30% tax-free allowance for the remainder of your original 5-year period. Select "Yes" on the toggle above to see this calculation.
</div>

<h2>Salary cap: the Balkenendenorm</h2>
<p>
From 2024 onwards, the 30% tax-free allowance can only be applied to the portion of your salary up to the so-called <em>Balkenendenorm</em> — the public sector salary norm for senior officials. In 2026 this cap is <strong>€233.000 gross per year</strong>.
</p>
<p>
If your annual salary exceeds €233.000, the ruling still applies, but only on the first €233.000. Any amount above the cap is fully subject to Dutch income tax at the highest rate (49.50%).
</p>
<p>
For most expats this cap has no practical impact, since the vast majority earn below this threshold.
</p>

<h2>How to apply for the 30% ruling</h2>
<p>The application must be submitted jointly by you and your Dutch employer to the Dutch Tax Authority (Belastingdienst). Here is the process step by step:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Start your job in the Netherlands.</strong> Your employment contract with a Dutch employer is a prerequisite.</li>
<li><strong>Gather your documents.</strong> You will need your employment contract, proof of address outside the Netherlands (16 of the last 24 months), degree certificates, and a copy of your passport.</li>
<li><strong>Submit the application together with your employer.</strong> Your employer files the request using the Belastingdienst's online portal or the relevant application form. <em>Apply within 4 months of your first working day</em> — late applications result in losing the benefit retroactively for those months.</li>
<li><strong>Receive the decision letter.</strong> The Belastingdienst typically responds within 10–13 weeks. The letter (beschikking) confirms the start date, end date, and conditions.</li>
<li><strong>Your employer applies the ruling in payroll.</strong> Once approved, your employer deducts 30% (or the applicable phase percentage) from your gross salary before calculating wage tax (loonbelasting). You do not need to do anything extra in your annual income tax return.</li>
</ol>

<div class="callout info">
<strong>Tip: switching employers</strong>
If you change jobs, the ruling does not automatically transfer. Your new Dutch employer must submit a new application together with you. There must be no employment gap longer than 3 months, or the ruling period restarts.
</div>

/**
* Calculate Dutch income tax (box 1, 2026) over taxable income.
* Brackets: 0–38441 @ 36.97%, 38441+ @ 49.50%
*/
function calcIncomeTax(income) {
if (income <= 0) return 0;
var BRACKET1_TOP = 38441;
var RATE1 = 0.3697;
var RATE2 = 0.4950;
var tax = 0;
if (income <= BRACKET1_TOP) {
tax = income * RATE1;
} else {
tax = BRACKET1_TOP * RATE1 + (income - BRACKET1_TOP) * RATE2;
}
return tax;
}

/**
* Algemene heffingskorting 2026
* Max €3.362 (tot €24.813), daarna afbouw 6.630% tot ~€75.518, daarna €0
*/
function calcGeneralCredit(income) {
var MAX_CREDIT = 3362;
var PHASE_UP_END = 24813;
var PHASE_OUT_START = 24813;
var PHASE_OUT_END = 75518;
var PHASE_OUT_RATE = 0.06630;

if (income <= 0) return 0;
if (income <= PHASE_UP_END) return MAX_CREDIT;
if (income >= PHASE_OUT_END) return 0;
var reduction = (income - PHASE_OUT_START) * PHASE_OUT_RATE;
return Math.max(0, MAX_CREDIT - reduction);
}

/**
* Arbeidskorting 2026
* Band 1: 0–11491   → 8.425%
* Band 2: 11491–24821 → 968 + 29.861% over excess
* Band 3: 24821–39958 → 4950 + 3.085% over excess
* Band 4: 39958–124935 → 5417 - 6.510% over excess
* Band 5: >124935 → 0
*/
function calcLabourCredit(income) {

var B1 = 11491, B2 = 24821, B3 = 39958, B4 = 124935;

return income * 0.08425;
} else if (income <= B2) {

} else if (income <= B3) {

}
}

return Math.max(0, netTax);
}
