## What the Kennismigrant Permit Actually Is

The *kennismigrant* (knowledge migrant or highly skilled migrant) residence permit is a category within Dutch immigration law designed for non-EU workers who are paid above a defined salary threshold and whose employer has been vetted and approved as a trusted sponsor by the IND (Immigratie- en Naturalisatiedienst).

This is not a job-seeker visa. You cannot apply for it independently. Your employer applies on your behalf, and only after an employment contract exists. That is a fundamentally different structure from, say, the orientation year visa for graduates, which you apply for yourself.

The permit gives you the right to live and work in the Netherlands for the duration of your employment contract, up to five years, and it ties you to your recognised-sponsor employer. You can change jobs — but your new employer must also be a recognised sponsor.

The permit is renewable and extendable, and it sits on a clear path toward permanent residency that is, by Dutch immigration standards, relatively predictable.

## The Recognised Sponsor System

This is the first checkpoint, and it trips people up more than any other part of the process.

The IND maintains a public list of *erkende referenten* — recognised sponsors — who have been vetted and approved to bring in knowledge migrants. The list includes most major Dutch universities, research institutions, large multinationals (ASML, Shell, Philips, ING, Booking.com, Adyen, and thousands of others), and many mid-sized companies that have gone through the approval process.

**If your employer is already on the list:** your application process is fast. Your employer submits through the IND's online employer portal, and processing takes 2–4 weeks under the knowledge-migrant fast track. You do not need to attend an IND appointment in most cases — HR handles the paperwork, you provide the documents.

**If your employer is not on the list:** they must first apply to become a recognised sponsor. This is a separate process that costs approximately €2,935 and takes 8–12 weeks. The company provides financial records, demonstrates the vacancy is genuine, and agrees to ongoing IND reporting obligations. Only after approval can they submit your kennismigrant application.

You can verify whether a specific employer is on the list via the <a href="https://ind.nl/en/highly-skilled-migrant" target="_blank" rel="noopener">IND's highly skilled migrant page</a>, which has a search function. Check this before accepting an offer from a smaller company — not because smaller companies are unreliable, but because you want to know whether you are looking at a 3-week timeline or a 14-week one.

## Salary Thresholds 2026

The IND sets salary thresholds annually. They apply to your gross monthly salary excluding the 8% *vakantiegeld* (holiday allowance) that Dutch employers pay on top of the monthly amount. Most Dutch contracts state the monthly salary and the vacation allowance separately — the threshold applies to the monthly figure only.

The approximate 2026 thresholds are:

| Age group | Monthly gross threshold (excl. 8% holiday allowance) |
|---|---|
| 30 years and older | €5,331 |
| Under 30 years | €3,909 |
| Orientation-year graduates (reduced threshold) | €2,801 |

The lower threshold for graduates applies specifically to people who have completed the Dutch *zoekjaar* (orientation year visa) and transition to a kennismigrant permit afterward. If you fall into this category — you have a Dutch master's degree or a master's from a top-200 world university — the significantly lower salary threshold opens up roles that would otherwise not qualify under the standard thresholds.

For everything on the orientation year and how it leads into the knowledge migrant route, see my [orientation year visa guide](/blog/orientation-year-visa-netherlands-2026/).

**An important nuance on what counts toward the threshold:** the IND checks your contractual base salary against the threshold. Allowances, bonuses, and reimbursements generally do not count. If your salary is just below the threshold — even €1 under — the application will be rejected. Some employers structure packages with housing, car, or relocation allowances that make the effective compensation much higher, but if the base salary in the contract is below threshold, the IND will not count the extras. Confirm this in writing with your HR department before submission.

## How the Application Works

The process runs through your employer, not through you personally. Here is the typical sequence from offer acceptance to permit in hand.

### Step 1: Employer prepares the application

Your employer (via HR or an immigration lawyer) assembles the package:
- A signed employment contract confirming the threshold-compliant salary
- Your valid passport (at least six months' validity beyond the intended residence period)
- A passport photo meeting IND specifications
- Proof of relevant qualifications if the specific role requires them
- The employer's recognised-sponsor credentials

### Step 2: Submission to the IND

The employer submits through the IND online employer portal (*Werkgeversportaal*). The application fee in 2026 is approximately €345, typically paid by the employer.

### Step 3: Processing

Under the fast-track procedure for recognised sponsors, the IND targets a 2-week turnaround, with most straightforward applications completed within 4 weeks. Complicated cases — previous immigration issues, incomplete documents, salary edge cases — can take longer.

During processing you will usually have no direct contact with the IND. Your employer's HR or their immigration lawyer is the point of contact.

### Step 4: MVV or status change

If approved and you are applying from outside the Netherlands, the IND issues an *machtiging tot voorlopig verblijf* (MVV) — a provisional residence visa — which you collect at the Dutch embassy or consulate in your home country before entering the Netherlands.

If you are already in the Netherlands under a valid permit (such as an orientation year or student permit), you can change status without leaving.

### Step 5: Collecting your residence permit card

After arriving in the Netherlands or completing the status change, you collect your *verblijfsvergunning* card at an IND desk. Book this appointment promptly — the card matters for banking, housing, and healthcare registration.

### Step 6: BSN registration

If you do not already have a BSN, register with your gemeente (municipality) within five days of arriving. The BSN is your citizen service number and is required for essentially everything — banking, healthcare, employment records, the tax system. ## The 30% Ruling: How It Combines With the Kennismigrant

The question I get most from incoming knowledge migrants: can I get the 30% ruling?

Short answer: yes, if you qualify — and many knowledge migrants do. The two schemes are explicitly designed to work together.

The **30% ruling** (*30%-regeling*) allows your employer to pay up to 30% of your gross salary as a tax-free allowance on the basis that you were recruited from abroad and have specific expertise that is scarce in the Dutch labour market. The effective tax saving is significant — a knowledge migrant on €6,000/month gross can net several hundred euros more per month with the ruling in place.

**Key conditions:**

- You must have lived more than 150 kilometres from the Dutch border for at least 16 of the 24 months before starting Dutch employment (the *afstandseis* — distance rule). Workers from border regions of Belgium and Germany sometimes do not qualify.
- Your employer must apply to the Belastingdienst within four months of your start date. Applications submitted late are processed, but apply only from the application date — not retrospectively. Missing this window by even a day costs you months of the benefit.
- Your salary must meet the separate minimum income threshold for the ruling itself (around €46,107 gross including the facility in 2026 for most roles).

**On the salary threshold interaction:** the IND checks your kennismigrant threshold against your full contractual gross salary. The 30% facility does not reduce the salary figure the IND sees. So a salary of €5,500/month gross qualifies for the kennismigrant threshold (€5,331) even if 30% is paid as a tax-free component. Both checks are independent.

The ruling runs for a maximum of five years under the post-2024 rules. ## Tax as a Knowledge Migrant

As an employee, your employer withholds income tax and social contributions (loonheffing) from every payslip. Most knowledge migrants do not need to do complex tax filings — but an annual return is still standard and often worth submitting to reclaim over-withheld amounts.

**Main reasons knowledge migrants file a return:**
- Reclaiming over-withheld tax from a partial first year (you were not resident the full year)
- Benefiting from the 30% ruling if your employer applied for it
- Claiming the mortgage interest deduction if you bought property
- Reporting foreign income or foreign bank balances above the *vermogensrendementsheffing* threshold

The Dutch tax year runs January to December. Returns are due between 1 March and 1 May of the following year. If you arrived mid-year, the return covers only the months of residence.

## Bringing Your Family

Your partner and children can join you in the Netherlands under a *gezinshereniging* (family reunification) application. This references your kennismigrant permit but is processed separately.

**What family members receive:**
- A residence permit tied to your status as a kennismigrant
- Full, unconditional work rights — no separate work permit (TWV) is required
- Access to Dutch public services, healthcare, schools, and social systems

**Who qualifies:**
- Spouse or registered partner (marriage or registration certificate required)
- Unmarried partner in a durable relationship (requires six-plus months of cohabitation evidence and relationship documentation — a more involved process)
- Dependent children under 18

**Income requirement:** The IND checks that your salary is sufficient to support the family. If you are already above the knowledge migrant salary threshold, this requirement is automatically met.

**Timing:** Family members can apply simultaneously with your initial kennismigrant application or separately afterward. Applying together adds administrative complexity but means everyone arrives authorised at the same time.

## Common Pitfalls

After helping many expats through this process, I keep seeing the same avoidable problems.

### Salary drops below threshold mid-permit

Your employer must maintain your salary at or above the threshold throughout the permit period. If your pay is cut — perhaps during a company restructuring or a performance conversation — and falls below the threshold, the kennismigrant permit becomes non-compliant. The employer must notify the IND. You typically get a window to resolve it (new role, salary restored) before the permit is revoked. But it is a genuinely precarious position.

This matters most at startups and scale-ups where compensation shifts more easily. Before accepting any pay reduction, however temporary, understand the immigration implications with your HR team and ideally an immigration lawyer.

### Job loss and the three-month window

Three months to find new qualifying employment is workable with an active network. It is very tight for a cold job search — especially because your new employer must also be a recognised sponsor, which adds filtering to your search.

When you lose your job, notify the IND immediately, start your search the same week, and use every resource available. See [how to find a job in the Netherlands as an expat](/blog/how-to-find-job-netherlands-expat-2026/) and [best job boards for expats Netherlands](/blog/best-job-boards-netherlands-expats-2026/) for practical search resources.

### Missing the 30% ruling four-month window

If your employer's HR department does not apply for the ruling within four months of your start date, you permanently lose the ability to apply it retrospectively. This can cost tens of thousands of euros in additional tax over five years. Make sure HR knows about the deadline from the contract stage. If you are joining a smaller company without experienced Dutch HR, raise it yourself in writing.

### Assuming the orientation-year reduced threshold applies

The €2,801/month reduced threshold applies only to people transitioning from an orientation year permit. If you completed a master's abroad and came directly to a Dutch employer without first doing a zoekjaar, the standard thresholds apply (€3,909 or €5,331 depending on age). Confirm which threshold applies to your specific situation before the contract is signed.

### Diploma recognition for regulated professions

The kennismigrant visa does not require your foreign diploma to be formally recognised in the Netherlands — the salary threshold does the qualifying work. But if you want to practice in a regulated profession (medicine, law, nursing, certain engineering roles), separate diploma recognition via Nuffic or the relevant professional body is required. ## Path to Permanent Residency

Plan for this from day one, even when it feels abstract on arrival.

After **five years of continuous legal residence** in the Netherlands, you can apply for:
- Dutch permanent residence (*verblijfsvergunning voor onbepaalde tijd*), or
- EU long-term resident status (valid across EU member states)

**Continuous** means no gaps longer than six consecutive months outside the Netherlands, and no lapses in residence permit validity. Short holidays, work travel, and family visits do not break continuity. Extended periods abroad do.

**Civic integration requirement:** you must pass the inburgering exam at A2 level or higher, unless exempt. Exemptions cover nationals of the EU, Australia, Canada, Japan, New Zealand, South Korea, Switzerland, and the United States, as well as holders of Dutch diplomas and people who completed integration under earlier rules. If you are not from an exempt country, start Dutch language preparation early — it takes more time than people expect. See [best Dutch language schools online](/blog/best-dutch-language-schools-online-2026/) and [best language app to learn Dutch quickly](/blog/best-language-app-to-learn-dutch-quickly-2026/).

**Previous residence counts:** if you were in the Netherlands for two years as a student and three years as a kennismigrant, those five years combined count toward the requirement. Keep records of all previous permits and residency dates.

## Renewing the Kennismigrant Permit

The kennismigrant permit is issued for the length of your employment contract, typically one to three years. When your contract extends or you move to a permanent contract, renew the permit through your employer via the IND employer portal.

Renewal timelines mirror the initial application — 2–4 weeks for recognised sponsors. Apply before the current permit expires. Processing a renewal late can create temporary status gaps that cause problems with banks, landlords, and health insurers.

A permanent employment contract does not automatically create an indefinite permit. You continue renewing the residence permit until you qualify for permanent residency after five years.

## Dutch Holiday Pay and Your Salary Package

One aspect of Dutch employment law that surprises many incoming knowledge migrants is *vakantiegeld* — holiday pay or vacation allowance. Dutch employers are legally required to pay an additional 8% of your annual gross salary as vacation allowance, typically in May or June each year, though some employers spread it monthly.

This 8% is on top of your monthly salary, not included in it. So when evaluating whether your salary meets the kennismigrant threshold, the IND looks at the monthly base amount, not the monthly amount plus a pro-rated share of the vacation allowance.

In practice: if your contract states €5,000/month, your employer also owes you €4,800/year in vacation allowance (8% × €60,000). Your gross annual package is €64,800. But the IND check uses €5,000 against the threshold, not €5,400. This distinction matters most when your salary sits close to the threshold — confirm exactly which figure appears in your contract as the monthly gross.

Dutch employment also comes with statutory minimum holiday entitlement of 20 days per year (four weeks) plus most employers give 25 days in professional roles. Your package offer should state both the salary and the leave entitlement clearly. ## Moving to Freelance or ZZP on a Kennismigrant Permit

A question I hear regularly: can I do freelance work on the side while holding a kennismigrant permit?

The permit ties you to your recognised-sponsor employer. Whether additional self-employed work is permitted depends on an annotation (*aantekening*) on your permit card. Many kennismigrant permits do permit secondary employment — check your permit documentation and the original IND letter. Some do not.

Transitioning fully from employed to self-employed is a different matter. When you leave employment, your kennismigrant permit lapses. You would need either a self-employment permit or, if you have five years of residence, to apply for permanent residency first.

If freelancing is part of your future plans, understand the ZZP route in advance. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the salary threshold for the highly skilled migrant visa in 2026?

The approximate IND 2026 thresholds (excluding the 8% holiday allowance) are: €5,331 per month for applicants aged 30 and over, €3,909 per month for those under 30, and €2,801 per month for graduates transitioning from a zoekjaar (orientation year) permit. The salary must appear as the base amount in your employment contract — allowances and bonuses generally do not count.

### Can my employer sponsor me for a kennismigrant visa?

Only employers on the IND recognised-sponsor list can file the application. Most large Dutch companies and universities are on the list. Smaller companies may need to apply for recognised-sponsor status first — a process taking 8–12 weeks and costing around €2,935. Check the IND list before accepting an offer so you know your timeline.

### Can I combine the kennismigrant visa with the 30% ruling?

Yes. The two schemes work together. Your employer applies for the ruling via the Belastingdienst within four months of your start date. The IND checks your full gross contractual salary against the kennismigrant threshold regardless of the ruling, so qualifying for it does not reduce the salary figure the IND sees. You must meet the distance rule and the ruling's own income threshold separately.

### What happens if I lose my job on a kennismigrant visa?

You have three months to find new qualifying employment at a recognised-sponsor employer. Your permit stays valid during this period. Notify the IND as soon as employment ends and begin the search immediately — three months moves fast when you also need to find a recognised sponsor.

### How long until I can get permanent residency?

Five years of continuous legal residence. You must also pass the civic integration (inburgering) exam at A2 level, unless exempt by nationality. Most knowledge migrants from Western countries are exempt. Earlier residence under student or orientation-year permits counts toward the five years.

### Can my family join me on a kennismigrant visa?

Yes. Spouse or partner and dependent children can join under family reunification. They receive a permit with full, unconditional work rights — no separate TWV needed. The income requirement is met automatically by the kennismigrant salary being above threshold.
