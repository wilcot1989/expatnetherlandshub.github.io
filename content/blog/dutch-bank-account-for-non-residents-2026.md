## Why Traditional Dutch Banks Cannot Help You (Yet)

ING, ABN AMRO, Rabobank, and SNS all require a BSN (Burgerservicenummer) before they will open a current account. The BSN is the Dutch citizen service number — it is assigned when you register at your local municipality (gemeente), which requires a confirmed Dutch address, which requires you to physically be in the Netherlands.

The [Dutch National Bank (DNB)](https://www.dnb.nl/en/) imposes anti-money-laundering identification standards that these banks implement by requiring a BSN. There is no workaround. If you try to book an appointment without one, you will be turned away or told to come back later.

Even after you get your BSN, the process takes time: appointment availability at large banks is often two to three weeks out. After the appointment, card delivery and activation takes another five to ten days. So the realistic timeline from arrival to having a working Dutch bank account at ING or ABN AMRO is four to six weeks minimum.

That is four to six weeks without receiving salary, without setting up direct debits for rent, and without a card that Dutch webshops fully accept.

The solution is to go fintech first, traditional bank later. Here is how.

## Option 3: Revolut — NL IBAN Opt-In

Revolut is probably the most widely used fintech app among expats globally. In the Netherlands, Revolut has introduced an NL IBAN opt-in that allows users registered at a Dutch address to receive a Dutch NL IBAN rather than the default Lithuanian (LT) IBAN.

**The important detail:** the NL IBAN opt-in on Revolut requires you to register with a Dutch address. Unlike Wise, Revolut cannot be opened with a completely non-Dutch setup and then magically give you a Dutch IBAN. You need to be in the Netherlands first (or at least have a Dutch address) and then switch to the NL IBAN from within the app.

**How to get the Revolut NL IBAN:**

1. Download Revolut and open an account (Standard is free).
2. Register with your Dutch address.
3. Go to Account → Personal Details → Account Details → Switch to Dutch IBAN.
4. The switch is processed within a few minutes.

If you already have a Revolut account from your home country, you can update your address once you arrive in the Netherlands and then apply for the NL IBAN switch.

**Cost:** Standard Revolut is free. Paid plans (Plus at ~€3.99/month, Premium at ~€9.99/month) add features like better exchange rate limits, travel insurance, and priority support.

The one catch with Revolut: it operates under a Lithuanian banking licence in the EU, and Dutch consumer protections and deposit guarantee schemes apply differently than with a fully Dutch-licensed bank. For most day-to-day use this does not matter, but it is worth knowing.

The same SEPA rules apply — all EU IBANs should be accepted — but the same edge-case problems with Dutch employers and automated direct-debit systems can occur with a DE IBAN as with a BE IBAN. N26 is best when Wise is already your primary account but you want a second account with a debit card from a fully licensed bank.

**Cost:** N26 Standard is free. N26 Smart (~€4.90/month) and N26 You (~€9.90/month) add premium features.

### You Are Starting a Dutch Employment Contract

1. Open Wise immediately (even weeks before departure) — use this for any initial payments.

## What Happens When You Get Your BSN?

Your BSN opens the door to traditional Dutch banking. Whether you actually need to switch depends on your situation.

**You probably need a traditional Dutch bank if:**
- You are applying for a Dutch mortgage (ABN AMRO and ING have in-house mortgage products requiring their accounts)
- Your employer specifically requires an ING or ABN AMRO account (uncommon, but some older Dutch companies still insist)
- You need a Dutch business bank account (Bunq Business is an option, but traditional banks offer more credit facilities)
- You want access to Dutch iDEAL payments in their full native form (though Bunq supports iDEAL)

**You can probably stay with Bunq + Wise if:**
- You are an employee receiving salary via SEPA transfer
- You pay rent and bills via bank transfer or direct debit
- You travel frequently and value good exchange rates
- You do not need credit products or a Dutch overdraft

If you do decide to open a traditional account, ABN AMRO and ING both allow online appointment booking and have English-language support for expats. The process takes about 30 minutes in-branch and another one to two weeks for card delivery.

## Common Mistakes Non-Residents Make

**1. Waiting until arrival to think about banking.**
The day you land is too late if your employer needs an account number in the first week. Open Wise before you book your flights.

**2. Assuming a BE or DE IBAN will definitely work for salary.**
Most of the time it does. But before your first payroll run, confirm with HR in writing that their payroll system accepts non-NL IBANs. If not, Bunq is your same-day fix.

**3. Giving traditional bank timelines to employers.**
If you tell HR "I will have an ING account in a week," you are wrong. ING requires a BSN, a gemeente appointment, a bank appointment, and card delivery. The realistic timeline is four to six weeks. Set expectations correctly, and give them a Bunq NL IBAN as a bridge.

**4. Using Revolut as your primary EUR account without switching to NL IBAN.**
If you open Revolut in your home country and move to the Netherlands without switching to the NL IBAN, your account will show a LT IBAN. This works for SEPA, but it causes even more friction than a BE or DE IBAN with Dutch-specific systems. Take five minutes to switch when you arrive.

**5. Ignoring BSN collection because you have Wise and "everything works."**
It does work — until you need to file Dutch taxes, claim healthcare benefits, or sign a mobile phone contract. Get your BSN as soon as possible after arrival. Wise and Bunq are bridges, not permanent replacements for proper Dutch registration. See the [BSN application guide](/blog/expat-bsn-application-guide-2026/) for a step-by-step walkthrough.

**6. Forgetting to update your address in the apps when you move.**
Bunq and Revolut in particular may ask for address verification periodically. Keeping your registered address current avoids account freezes.

## The Honest Trade-off Summary

Every option here has a real limitation. Wise gives you a non-NL IBAN. Bunq requires a Dutch address to work smoothly. Revolut requires you to be in the Netherlands before you can get the NL IBAN. N26 gives you a DE IBAN that has similar edge-case problems to Wise.

There is no single perfect answer for every situation, which is why I almost always recommend the same two-step approach: **open Wise first** (remotely, before anything else), **open Bunq second** (the moment you have a Dutch address). Use Wise for international transfers and multi-currency needs; use Bunq for your NL IBAN day-to-day.

This combination covers about 95% of expat banking needs in the Netherlands without requiring a BSN or a visit to a Dutch bank branch.
