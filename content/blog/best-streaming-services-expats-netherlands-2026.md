## What changes when you become a Dutch resident

Three things happen the moment you become a Dutch resident:

**Your streaming libraries shrink and shift**. EU geo-restrictions reshape what you can watch. The Netherlands Netflix library is different from the US, UK, German, or French library. Some shows arrive late, some never arrive at all.

**Sports rights get reshuffled**. UK Sky and BT Sport do not work here. Dutch sports rights are split between Viaplay, Ziggo Sport, and ESPN NL. Your favourite sport may be on a service you have never heard of.

**Local services emerge**. Videoland, NPO Plus, NLZIET — Dutch-specific services with strong local content but limited expat utility unless you are learning the language.

The expat-friendly answer is to mix global services with one or two Dutch-specific ones, plus a VPN for content that did not follow you here.

## My ranking of streaming services for expats in 2026

After three years of testing, here is how I rank the main options for English-speaking expats.

### 1. Netflix — still the default

Netherlands Netflix has around 5,500 titles. The mix is heavy on US originals, European drama, K-content, and global hits. The Dutch dub-and-subtitle handling is excellent.

What works:
- Most Netflix originals are available globally
- Strong European drama (Money Heist, Lupin, Babylon Berlin)
- Reliable subtitle and audio language switching
- Family profile and kids content

What is missing:
- Many US originals from Hulu, Peacock, Paramount+ (those services do not operate here directly)
- HBO classics moved to HBO Max
- Disney content moved to Disney+

Plans for 2026 in the Netherlands:
- Standard with ads: around €6 per month
- Standard: around €14 per month
- Premium (4K, 4 screens): around €20 per month

For most expats, the standard plan is enough. Standard with ads is fine if you do not mind 4 to 5 minutes of ads per hour.

### 2. Disney+ — best for families and Marvel/Star Wars fans

Disney+ in the Netherlands has nearly the same library as the US, with some local content additions. For families with kids it is essential. For Marvel, Star Wars, and Pixar fans it is non-negotiable.

Plans for 2026:
- Standard with ads: around €6 per month
- Standard: around €11 per month
- Premium: around €15 per month
- Annual: around €110 (Standard) — saves 17%

### 3. HBO Max — strongest for prestige series

HBO Max launched in the Netherlands in 2024 and now has the full HBO catalogue plus Warner Bros films. Game of Thrones, House of the Dragon, Succession, The Last of Us, The White Lotus — all here.

Plans for 2026:
- Standard with ads: around €6 per month
- Standard: around €11 per month
- Premium: around €14 per month

For prestige drama fans this is the best single subscription in the Dutch market. The catalogue is deeper than Netflix on the quality axis.

### 4. Viaplay — the sports power player

Viaplay holds Dutch broadcasting rights for Premier League (until 2028), Bundesliga, Formula 1, and significant tennis and combat sports. If you watch any of these, you need Viaplay.

Plans for 2026:
- Total: around €18 per month
- Annual: around €170 — saves 21%

The non-sports content (Nordic noir, original drama) is decent but not the main reason most subscribers pay.

### 5. Videoland — Dutch-language, strong for language learners

RTL's Videoland is the Dutch counterpart to Hulu. Strong reality, Dutch drama, local films, and a rotating selection of imported series. For expats actively learning Dutch, this is the single best service for immersion.

Plans for 2026:
- Basic: around €4 per month
- Plus: around €7 per month
- Premium: around €10 per month

If you are not learning Dutch, Videoland is skippable.

### 6. NLZIET and NPO Plus — public broadcasting

NLZIET aggregates the major Dutch commercial channels with catch-up. NPO Plus is the ad-free version of the public broadcaster's catch-up service. Both are very Dutch-focused.

Useful only for serious Dutch language learners or expats following local news, politics, and sports beyond what Viaplay covers.

### 7. Apple TV+ — a strong handful of originals

Apple TV+ has fewer titles than its rivals but the originals tend to be high-quality (Ted Lasso, Severance, Slow Horses, Foundation). At €11 per month it is hard to recommend as a primary service but as a third or fourth subscription it works.

### 8. Amazon Prime Video — bundled with Prime, decent originals

Prime Video in the Netherlands is included with Amazon Prime (around €5 per month or €50 per year). The library is mid-tier — strong on originals like The Boys, Reacher, and Wheel of Time, but thin on catalogue depth. The Prime shipping benefit makes the subscription worth it for many expats regardless of the streaming.

## Watching home country content from the Netherlands

This is where most expats hit problems. Solutions vary by region.

**UK content (BBC iPlayer, ITVX, Channel 4)**: All geo-blocked outside the UK. EU portability does not apply because the UK is not in the EU. A UK-IP VPN restores access. Free VPNs are universally blocked; paid streaming-optimised VPNs work most of the time.

**US content (Hulu, Peacock, US Netflix, Paramount+)**: All geo-blocked. A US-IP VPN restores access. Hulu requires a US billing address for new sign-ups; an existing US Hulu account works fine via VPN.

**Australian content (ABC iView, 9Now, Stan)**: Geo-blocked. AU-IP VPN restores access.

**German content (RTL+, Joyn, ARD/ZDF)**: Mostly geo-blocked but RTL+ has limited EU portability for existing subscribers.

## Common expat streaming mistakes

**Sticking with home country subscriptions and using VPN permanently**. The major services have improved their VPN detection. Periodic blocks are now common. You also remain on home-country billing currency with FX fees.

**Subscribing to too many services**. Five subscriptions at €11 to €14 each adds up fast. Most households genuinely use two or three.

**Forgetting to cancel**. Auto-renewal is ubiquitous. Set calendar reminders one month before annual renewal.

**Ignoring the ad-supported tiers**. The €6 ad-supported tiers are a 50% discount with relatively modest ad load. For casual viewing they are perfectly fine.

**Paying full price during launch promotions**. New services frequently offer 50% off for the first 3 to 6 months. HBO Max, Disney+, and Viaplay have all run such promos in the past two years.

## My honest recommendation for new expats

Start with one paid service in your first month. Netflix is the safe default. Add a VPN if you want home country access. After a month, decide based on what you actually watched whether to add Disney+, HBO Max, or Viaplay.

Avoid signing up for everything in the first week. The Dutch market has more options than most countries and the temptation to recreate your entire home country setup is strong. Resist it. €40 per month on streaming you do not watch is real money.
