## What the Orientation Year Visa Is

The *zoekjaar hoogopgeleiden* — literally "search year for highly educated persons" — is a Dutch residence permit that gives recent international graduates one calendar year to find employment in the Netherlands. During that year you have no work restrictions at all. Any job, any sector, any employment form including self-employment.

The rationale behind the visa is simple: the Netherlands wants to attract and retain internationally educated talent. Rather than requiring graduates to secure an employer before arriving, the orientation year gives them time to job search while being physically present in the country. Being present is a real advantage — Dutch hiring culture is heavily relationship and network based, and being locally available for face-to-face interviews, networking events, and informal conversations makes a significant difference compared to applying remotely from abroad.

The visa is a bridge, not an endpoint. The goal is to use the year to secure employment that qualifies for a kennismigrant (knowledge migrant or highly skilled migrant) permit, which then gives you ongoing legal residence tied to that employment. If you find work during the orientation year, the transition to a kennismigrant permit is one of the smoothest processes in the Dutch immigration system — and because you are transitioning from a zoekjaar, you benefit from a reduced salary threshold of approximately €2,801/month rather than the standard €3,909 or €5,331.

## Who Qualifies

The qualification rules are specific. Getting this wrong at the application stage is the most common avoidable mistake.

**You qualify if you hold:**

1. A master's degree from a Dutch university (universiteit or hogeschool at master's level), OR
2. A master's degree from a university that appears in the top 200 of at least one of the following recognised global rankings:
   - Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings
   - QS World University Rankings
   - Academic Ranking of World Universities (ARWU / Shanghai Rankings)

**AND you apply within three years of your graduation date as stated on your diploma.**

Let me expand on each of these points because the details matter.

### What counts as a master's degree

A master's degree (typically 1–2 years after a bachelor's). Bachelor's degrees alone do not qualify for the zoekjaar. A postgraduate certificate or diploma is not the same as a master's. If your qualification is called something other than "master" in your home country's educational system, check with the IND or a *Nuffic*-affiliated assessor whether it is treated as equivalent.

Dutch universities operate two types of master's programmes relevant here: the *universitaire mastersopleiding* (university master's) and the *hbo-master* (university of applied sciences master's). Both can qualify for the zoekjaar, though the hbo route has some specific conditions. If your Dutch degree is at an hogeschool, verify with the IND directly before applying.

### The top-200 ranking requirement for non-Dutch degrees

The rankings are checked against the edition of the list current at the time of your graduation, not the current year's list. So if you graduated from a university that was ranked top 200 in the year of your graduation but has since dropped, you still qualify. Keep a copy of the relevant ranking page from your graduation year — you may need to provide this as evidence.

If your university ranks in the top 200 in any one of the three ranking systems (THE, QS, or Shanghai), that is sufficient. You do not need to be in the top 200 on all three.

### The three-year window

This is the one that catches people out most often. You must submit your orientation year application within three years of the graduation date printed on your diploma. Not three years from when you stopped studying, not three years from the ceremony, but three years from the official date on the diploma.

If you graduated in June 2022 and are reading this in August 2025, you have until June 2025 — which means you have potentially already missed the window. Check your diploma date immediately if you are considering applying.

The three-year rule applies whether you apply from within the Netherlands or from abroad.

## What You Can Do During the Orientation Year

The zoekjaar permit has genuinely no work restriction. This is unusual in the Dutch immigration system and worth being explicit about.

During the orientation year you can:

- Work as an employee in any role, skilled or unskilled, for any employer, without the employer needing to be a recognised sponsor
- Work self-employed as a ZZP (sole proprietor) without a separate self-employment permit
- Start a BV (private limited company) — though this requires meeting the DGA salary requirements and involves more setup
- Work part-time, full-time, or a combination
- Take any paid internship or traineeship
- Work in multiple jobs simultaneously
- Take unpaid work or volunteer while also doing paid work

What you cannot do is violate other immigration conditions — overstay, engage in criminal activity, or claim social benefits you are not entitled to. The standard residence permit rules apply. You also cannot vote in national elections and you do not have the same social rights as an EU citizen.

The freedom to work any job is intentional. Many zoekjaar holders take lower-level employment initially — retail, hospitality, office work — to generate income while conducting their real job search in parallel. This is a legitimate and common strategy. The income from part-time or lower-skilled work covers rent and living costs without burning through savings while the actual career search runs alongside it.

## What the Orientation Year Does Not Give You

A few things people assume and then discover are incorrect:

**No 30% ruling during the zoekjaar.** The 30% ruling requires a kennismigrant permit (or certain other qualifying permits) with a recognised-sponsor employer. During the orientation year itself, you are on a different permit category. The ruling cannot be activated for this period. Once you transition to kennismigrant employment, your new employer applies for the ruling within four months of your start date. For the ruling specifics, see the [best tax advisor for 30% ruling guide](/blog/best-tax-advisor-30-percent-ruling-2026/).

**No extension.** The orientation year is exactly one year. There is no mechanism to extend it. If your year ends before you have secured qualifying employment, your permit expires and you need to leave or switch to a different category. This is not a cliff you should discover at month eleven.

**No guarantee of transitioning to kennismigrant.** Finding a qualifying job during the zoekjaar is on you. The permit does not reserve a kennismigrant slot — it just gives you the time and legal right to search. If no qualifying offer materialises, you are in the same position as anyone else who needs to leave.

**No right to bring family initially.** The zoekjaar is a personal permit. Family members (partner, children) cannot join you on a family reunification basis tied to the orientation year. Once you transition to a kennismigrant permit, family reunification becomes available. During the zoekjaar year, family members would need their own independent basis for Dutch residence.

## The IND Application: Documents Required

Getting the documentation right the first time saves weeks. The IND will not process an incomplete application, and returning to gather missing certified translations adds delay and cost.

**If applying from within the Netherlands (status change from student permit or other valid permit):**

- Valid passport with at least six months' validity beyond the intended residence period
- Passport photos meeting IND specifications (check the current requirements on the IND website — they are specific)
- Official master's diploma or graduation certificate (original or certified copy)
- Official transcript of records showing the qualification was completed (not just enrolled)
- If your degree is from a non-Dutch institution, evidence of the university's top-200 ranking at the time of your graduation — a screenshot or archived version of the relevant ranking list with the year clearly shown
- Completed IND application form 7507 (download from the IND website — use the current version, not an older cached one)
- Proof of sufficient income or financial means to support yourself during the year (savings, a scholarship, a job offer, or parental support documentation — the IND wants to see you have resources)
- Proof of Dutch address (from gemeente registration)

**If applying from abroad (MVV application at Dutch embassy/consulate):**

The document list is essentially the same, but submitted at the Dutch diplomatic post in your country of residence. After MVV approval, you travel to the Netherlands and then collect the zoekjaar residence permit card at an IND desk, which you must do within three months of the MVV date.

**Translation requirements:** Documents not in Dutch, English, German, or French typically need certified translation into one of these languages. Certified translators in the Netherlands are listed at the KBvT register. Budget €50–€120 per document for certified translation.

**Certified copies vs originals:** The IND generally accepts certified copies for diploma and transcript — certified by a notary or the institution itself. Check the current IND guidance before submitting, as requirements are updated periodically.

## The Application Fee and Timeline

The IND fee for the orientation year visa in 2026 is approximately **€243**. This is paid at the time of application submission. The fee is non-refundable even if the application is rejected.

**Processing times:**
- Within Netherlands (status change): typically 3–8 weeks
- From abroad (MVV application): 3–8 weeks, then your residence permit card collection after arriving

The IND does not have a fast-track option for orientation year applications in the same way that employer-sponsored kennismigrant applications have fast-track processing. Processing time depends on application volume and completeness of your documentation. Applying with any missing documents resets the clock.

Apply as early as possible — do not wait until your student permit expires or your last week in the Netherlands. If you are currently on a student permit, apply for the zoekjaar before the student permit expires. There is usually a period of grace after the permit expiry if a renewal or change application is pending, but do not rely on this being smooth.

## Applying While Already in the Netherlands

If you completed your degree at a Dutch institution and your student permit is still valid, you apply for the zoekjaar from within the Netherlands as a status change. You submit the application before your student permit expires and remain in legal status while the IND processes it.

The practical steps:
1. Confirm your graduation date is within the last three years
2. Gather your documents (diploma, transcript, ID photos, proof of income/savings, proof of address, form 7507)
3. Submit online via the IND website or by post (check the current submission method — the IND has been moving toward online submissions)
4. Pay the €243 fee
5. Receive a decision by post or via the IND's portal
6. If approved, collect your residence permit card at an IND desk (book the appointment in advance — IND desks in Amsterdam and other major cities require booking weeks ahead)

If you have already graduated, already left the Netherlands, and are now applying from abroad, the process goes through the Dutch embassy or consulate in your country of residence. You apply for an MVV (provisional residence visa), wait for approval, then travel to the Netherlands and collect the zoekjaar permit card from an IND desk within three months.

## BSN Registration

You need your *Burgerservicenummer* (BSN) — the Dutch citizen service number — to do almost everything: open a bank account, sign a rental contract, register with a GP, access healthcare, and eventually integrate into the Dutch employment system.

Register at your local gemeente (municipality) as soon as you have a Dutch address. In the larger cities (Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Utrecht, The Hague, Eindhoven) you must book an appointment in advance — walk-ins are not accommodated and appointments fill weeks ahead. In smaller municipalities the wait is shorter.

Bring your passport, your residence permit card or the IND approval letter if the card has not arrived yet, and proof of your Dutch address (rental contract, landlord's statement, or letter from your accommodation provider).

## Tax During the Orientation Year

If you earn income during the orientation year — employed or self-employed — you pay Dutch income tax.

As an employee, your employer withholds *loonheffing* (payroll tax) from your salary. You file a tax return for the relevant year between 1 March and 1 May of the following year. If you only worked part of the year, you may have over-withheld tax to reclaim.

As a ZZP (sole proprietor), you pay BTW (VAT) quarterly and income tax annually on your business profit. Even if you start working during the orientation year and your employer is a recognised sponsor, the ruling is tied to the kennismigrant permit. The moment you transition to kennismigrant employment, your employer should apply for the ruling within four months of your new employment start date. ## The Transition to Kennismigrant

The whole point of the zoekjaar is to find employment that qualifies for the kennismigrant permit. When you do, the transition is one of the smoother processes in the Dutch immigration system.

**The lower salary threshold:** because you are transitioning from a zoekjaar, the kennismigrant salary threshold is approximately **€2,801/month** (2026 approximate figure, excluding the 8% holiday allowance) rather than the standard €3,909 for under-30s or €5,331 for 30-and-over. This is a significant difference — it opens up entry-level professional roles that would not meet the standard threshold.

**The process:**
1. You find a qualifying employer (who must be a recognised sponsor — check the IND list)
2. The employer submits a kennismigrant application on your behalf via the IND employer portal
3. Your current zoekjaar permit is referenced in the application
4. Processing takes 2–4 weeks under the fast track
5. Your new kennismigrant permit is issued

**Timing matters:** you do not need to wait until the zoekjaar expires to transition. As soon as you have a qualifying job offer and the employer submits the application, you can begin the status change. If your zoekjaar is running out and you have an offer in hand, submit the kennismigrant application immediately — do not wait.

If your zoekjaar expires before the kennismigrant application is decided, you technically fall into an administrative gap. The IND generally allows you to remain in status during a pending permit application submitted before the previous permit expired, but this is not something to rely on. Time the transition so applications overlap, not so they run sequentially.

## Practical Job Search Strategy During the Year

The orientation year gives you twelve months. Treat the first month as setup (BSN, bank account, housing if needed, LinkedIn profile refreshed for the Dutch market) and the remaining eleven as active search — not eight months of passive applications followed by a three-month panic.

**Dutch hiring is relationship-heavy.** Cold applications with no prior connection have lower conversion rates here than in many other countries. Attend industry events, join professional associations relevant to your field, use your university alumni network actively, and reach out to people in your target companies for informational conversations before you need a job urgently.

**LinkedIn is genuinely important in the Netherlands.** Dutch professionals use it actively for both posting and reading. A strong LinkedIn profile with your degree, skills, and a clear professional story in English (Dutch is a bonus, not mandatory for most international roles) makes a real difference.

**Dutch CV norms differ from most other countries.** No photo in most sectors (unlike some European countries), clean format, dates explicit, Dutch companies appreciate brevity. ## What Happens if You Do Not Find Qualifying Employment

If you reach the end of the orientation year without a kennismigrant-qualifying job, your permit expires. At that point:

- **You must leave the Netherlands** unless you have another valid basis for residence
- **ZZP / self-employment permit** is an option if you have a credible business plan — this is a separate application route through the IND with different requirements
- **Re-applying for the zoekjaar** is not an option — the permit is once per qualification
- **Partner's residence** could be a basis if your partner has their own Dutch residence permit that covers family members

Not finding a qualifying job is genuinely common in certain sectors. Fields like hospitality, arts, NGO work, and parts of education often do not reach the €2,801 salary threshold even for professional roles. Be clear-eyed early in the year about whether your target roles will qualify on salary, and adjust your search accordingly if they do not.

The orientation year is not the only path into Dutch employment — there are also sector-specific permits, intra-company transfer routes, and EU Blue Card provisions — but the zoekjaar is the most flexible for graduates, and transitioning to kennismigrant is by far the most straightforward outcome.
