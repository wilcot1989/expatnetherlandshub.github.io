## How International Moving to the Netherlands Actually Works

Before comparing companies, you need to understand what you are buying. International moving has three layers:

**Origin services.** Packing, loading, export customs documentation, and transport to the port or border.

**Freight.** The actual movement of your goods — by truck for European moves, by container ship for intercontinental ones, occasionally by air.

**Destination services.** Customs clearance in the Netherlands, inland transport to your address, unloading, and (if booked) unpacking.

Most reputable international movers offer all three as a "door-to-door" package. Cheaper "port-to-port" quotes leave Dutch customs and final delivery as your problem — which can save money if you have time and patience, or cost you a fortune in storage and brokerage fees if you don't.

## What I Looked For

I evaluated the major movers serving the Netherlands on six criteria:

- **FIDI/FAIM accreditation.** This is the international moving industry's gold standard. Members are audited every three years on financial stability, claims handling, and operational quality.
- **English-language customer service.** You'd be amazed how many "international" movers default to local language only.
- **TOR (Transfer of Residence) experience.** Dutch customs is generally efficient, but only if forms are filed correctly. Movers who do this every week beat ones who do it occasionally.
- **Insurance options.** Real full-value replacement coverage, not just minimal carrier liability.
- **Track record on damages and timelines.** Based on Trustpilot, Google reviews, and expat forum feedback over 12+ months.
- **Transparent pricing.** No "low-ball quote, demand more on collection day" tactics.

## The Best International Moving Companies for the Netherlands in 2026

### 1. Crown Worldwide

Crown is one of the largest international movers globally and has a substantial Dutch operation in Amsterdam. They handle around 100,000 corporate and private moves a year, which means processes are mature and customs paperwork is rarely a problem.

Their strength is corporate relocations — if your employer is paying, Crown is almost certainly on the approved supplier list. For private clients, the trade-off is that you are buying premium service at premium prices. Quotes typically come in 15–25% above mid-market competitors.

What you get for the premium: dedicated move coordinator, full-service packing with proper export crating for fragile items, careful inventory management, and reliable timelines. Their claims handling is genuinely good — friends who had a piano damaged in transit had it repaired by an approved restorer within six weeks.

**Best for:** Corporate-paid moves, families with significant valuables, moves involving art or antiques.

### 2. AGS Movers

AGS is a French-headquartered network with a strong Dutch presence in Hoofddorp. They are FIDI/FAIM Plus accredited and consistently rate well in expat communities for European moves in particular.

Pricing is mid-market — typically 10–15% below Crown for comparable service. Their quote process is straightforward, and they have a reputation for sticking to estimates without surprise charges.

The weakness I've heard most often is communication delays during the actual transit phase. Once your shipment is in transit, updates can be infrequent. You sometimes have to chase them rather than receive proactive status updates.

**Best for:** Moves within Europe, Africa, and the Middle East. Reasonable quality at mid-market pricing.

### 3. Allied Pickfords / Allied Van Lines

Allied is a global network with strong representation in the UK, US, Canada, and Australia, plus Dutch agents in Amsterdam and Rotterdam. The brand operates as Pickfords in the UK and as Allied Van Lines in North America.

Quality varies somewhat by origin agent — the UK Pickfords operation is generally excellent, the Australian network is solid, and US agents range from very good to mediocre depending on which local franchise handles your origin services.

For US-to-Netherlands moves, ask specifically which origin agent will handle your move and search reviews for that specific local office.

**Best for:** UK-to-Netherlands moves and Australia-to-Netherlands moves where the origin Allied operation is reliable.

### 4. Sirelo / Boekhoorn-Sirelo

Sirelo is a Netherlands-based comparison platform that gets you binding quotes from multiple Dutch and international movers. They're particularly useful for expats already in the Netherlands moving onward, or for shorter European moves.

Worth using because: you submit details once and get 5+ quotes, which lets you triangulate fair pricing. Worth being cautious about: some of the smaller movers in their network have spotty reputations. Always check the FIDI/FAIM register and Trustpilot for any company before booking.

**Best for:** Quick European moves, comparison-shopping pricing benchmarks.

### 5. Asian Tigers Mobility

If you are moving from Hong Kong, Singapore, Bangkok, Taipei, or other Asian hubs, Asian Tigers is the specialist. They handle the origin side better than any of the global generalists for Asian origins, including the niche customs and export-license requirements that catch generalist movers off guard.

Their Dutch destination partner network varies — confirm in advance who will be handling Dutch customs and final delivery, ideally one of the big four Dutch movers.

**Best for:** Moves from Asian countries to the Netherlands.

### 6. UTS Hollander Mertz / De Haan Worldwide

Both are Dutch-headquartered FIDI/FAIM accredited movers with strong reputations within the Dutch market. They handle inbound moves to the Netherlands frequently and have well-established TOR customs processes.

Their international origin networks are smaller than the global giants, which can mean origin services come from a partner rather than an owned operation. For European origins this is usually fine; for intercontinental origins, ask who the actual origin partner will be.

**Best for:** Moves where you want a Dutch destination operator who knows local customs and delivery quirks.

### 7. SDV / Bolloré Logistics

Premium freight forwarder with a major office in Rotterdam. They are stronger on the freight and customs side than on the home-services (packing, unloading) side, where they typically partner with local Dutch movers.

Useful when your shipment is unusual — vehicles, large machinery, art collections — or when you are coordinating multiple shipments at once.

**Best for:** Complex shipments, vehicle imports, art and large items.

## Companies I'd Avoid

I'm not naming specific names because reputations shift, but the patterns to avoid are:

- **No FIDI/FAIM accreditation.** Many low-cost movers cut corners on insurance and process.
- **Quotes 30%+ below the mid-market.** This usually signals either misclassified shipments (you'll be charged more later) or operational shortcuts that produce damages and delays.
- **No physical presence in the Netherlands.** Pure-broker operations sub-contract everything and have limited accountability.
- **Pressure to sign within 24 hours.** Reputable movers give you time to compare and ask questions.
- **Demands for full payment upfront in cash or wire transfer to a non-corporate account.** This is a fraud red flag.

## Hidden Costs to Budget For

**Storage at origin or destination.** If your Dutch home isn't ready when your shipment arrives, you'll need storage. €150–€300 per month for a typical household, billed by the destination mover.

**Demurrage.** If your container sits at the port too long during customs, port authorities charge demurrage. Usually €100+ per day after the free-time window.

**TV and electronics import declaration.** Some specific items require additional Dutch declarations.

**Plant and food import restrictions.** Most cannot enter the EU. Don't ship them — they will be confiscated and you'll be billed for disposal.

**Deep cleaning of the empty old place and new place.** Often forgotten in moving budgets, easily €200–€400 per property.

**Temporary accommodation gap.** Shipments rarely arrive on the day you move in. Budget 1–4 weeks of temporary housing — see [Short-stay furnished housing](/blog/short-stay-furnished-housing-netherlands-2026/) for options.

## How to Get Comparable Quotes

The easiest way to triangulate fair pricing is to request quotes from three movers using identical scope:

1. **Same itemization.** Use the same room-by-room inventory for each quote. If you tell one mover "approximately 30 boxes" and another "120 specific items," you'll get incomparable numbers.
2. **Same service level.** Door-to-door full-service from all, or door-to-door with self-packing from all.
3. **Same insurance basis.** All quotes including full-value replacement at the same declared value.
4. **Same dates.** Pricing varies a lot by week — quote the same target dates from each mover.

For pre-survey, modern movers offer video walkthroughs via WhatsApp or a similar app. This is faster than in-person surveys and just as accurate for most households.

## What to Do in the 30 Days Before Your Move

**4 weeks out:** Confirm collection date, finalize inventory, declutter aggressively (every cubic meter you don't ship saves €100–€300).

**3 weeks out:** Book temporary accommodation in the Netherlands if shipping ETA is later than your arrival. [HousingAnywhere](https://go.expatnetherlandshub.com/housing-anywhere?ref=/blog/best-international-moving-companies-netherlands-2026/) is what I used for short-term furnished rentals.

**2 weeks out:** Notify utilities, banks, doctors. Start your [BSN application](/blog/expat-bsn-application-guide-2026/) preparation.

**1 week out:** Pack carry-luggage essentials separately (3+ weeks of clothing, important documents, prescriptions, chargers). Confirm Dutch arrival logistics, including [BSN registration](/blog/expat-bsn-application-guide-2026/) and [setting up a Dutch bank account](/blog/best-bank-account-expats-netherlands-2026/).

**Move day:** Walk the inventory with the lead packer. Photograph everything that goes into boxes. Don't sign the inventory until you've reviewed it.

## After Your Things Arrive

**Inspect immediately.** You typically have 7 days to file claims. Document any damage with photos before signing the delivery receipt.

**Don't unpack the kitchen first.** Unpack one room at a time so you can keep track of what's there and what isn't.

**Save the original packing materials** for at least the first six months in case anything turns out to be defective and needs to go back.

**Register your address change** with the gemeente within 5 days to keep your residency in good standing.

## Special Cases Worth Calling Out

### Pets

If you're shipping pets along with household goods, this is almost always a separate logistics flow. International pet relocation specialists (Animal Express, Jet Pets, IPATA-registered movers) handle veterinary documentation, EU pet passport / health certificates, IATA-compliant crating, and door-to-door delivery. Costs run €800–€2,500 for a single pet from Europe, €2,500–€5,000+ from intercontinental origins. Don't try to bundle pets with your household mover unless they explicitly offer pet relocation as a specialty — it almost always goes wrong.

For full pet-specific planning, see my [pet relocation to the Netherlands](/blog/pet-relocation-netherlands-2026/) guide.

### Vehicles

Importing a car to the Netherlands is its own complex process: BPM (vehicle import tax), RDW registration, technical inspection, MOT-equivalent certification, and (for non-EU origins) customs duties. International movers usually don't ship cars unless they have a specialty division. Specialized vehicle shippers (K Line, Wallenius Wilhelmsen, Höegh Autoliners) handle ocean transport.

Bringing your existing car often doesn't pay financially — the BPM and modification costs frequently exceed the resale value differential vs. selling at origin and buying in NL. Exception: classic, rare, or sentimental vehicles where the financial argument doesn't dominate.

### Art and Antiques

For valuable art collections (€50K+), specialty fine-art movers (Crozier, Art Move) provide climate-controlled transport, condition reports, and museum-grade insurance. Add 2–4x standard household-mover pricing. For modest collections (one or two valuable pieces), a quality mainstream mover with proper crating and full-value insurance is usually adequate.

### Wine Cellars

Many international movers refuse wine due to customs and breakage liability. Specialty wine logistics companies handle EU import declarations and temperature-controlled transport. Expect €10–€25 per bottle for door-to-door including paperwork. Often, the math says "drink it before you leave."

## Avoiding Moving Scams and Disputes

International moving has a long history of disputes — disappearing companies, surprise bills mid-transit, "lost" valuable items. Protect yourself:

**Demand a written binding estimate.** Not "estimated" — binding. Reputable movers commit to a price based on a documented inventory.

**Pay incrementally, not upfront.** Standard structure: 25–35% deposit at booking, 35–50% on origin loading, balance on destination delivery and acceptance. Avoid full-upfront-payment demands.

**Use a credit card or escrow** for the deposit where possible — gives you chargeback rights if the company disappears.

**Document everything.** Photos of every item before packing, time-stamped. Inventory lists signed and counter-signed.

**Keep originals of customs paperwork.** Don't hand the only copy of TOR documentation to the mover — keep your own copies.

**Verify FIDI/FAIM membership directly** on the FIDI website, not just on the mover's site.

If problems arise: FIDI has a dispute resolution mechanism for member companies. Use it before going to court — it's faster and cheaper.

## What I'd Do Differently Next Time

Looking back on my last international move, three things I'd change:

1. **Get five quotes, not three.** The price spread surprised me — top quote was 65% higher than bottom for identical scope.
2. **Insist on video-survey for accuracy.** In-person surveys are slower and less detailed than a focused video walkthrough with the survey lead.
3. **Pack jewelry, important documents, and irreplaceables in carry-on luggage.** Not the shipment. Items that go missing in shipment are usually unrecoverable.

Final perspective: international moving is one of the few life events where paying for quality genuinely pays back. The cheapest mover almost always becomes the most expensive once you factor in damaged items, customs delays, and stress costs.
