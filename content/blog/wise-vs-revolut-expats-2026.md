## What Each One Actually Is

**Wise** (formerly TransferWise) is a UK-origin financial services company licensed in Belgium for EU operations. Founded in 2011 specifically to disrupt international transfers, its core product is sending money across currencies at the real mid-market exchange rate plus a transparent fixed fee. It later added the multi-currency account, debit card, and Wise Business. Roughly 16 million customers in 2026.

**Revolut** is a UK-origin financial services company holding a Lithuanian banking license through Revolut Bank UAB. Started as a travel FX card, expanded into a full "financial super-app" — banking, FX, stocks, crypto, savings, insurance, eSIM, travel deals. Roughly 50 million customers in 2026.

The fundamental difference: Wise is a transfer specialist that grew sideways. Revolut is a feature platform that started with FX. Both got good at the other's home territory, but the DNA still shows.

### Pattern

For major currency pairs (EUR/USD, EUR/GBP, EUR/CHF) on weekdays during business hours, both are similar — Revolut sometimes slightly cheaper for free-tier users. For exotic currencies, weekend transfers, or any transfer above ~€2,000, **Wise is consistently cheaper**. The Revolut weekend markup is the single biggest gotcha.

## IBAN Reality

This is where the picture changed in late 2024.

**Wise:** issues a Belgian IBAN (BE...) for EUR by default. SEPA-compliant, accepted by most Dutch institutions, but historically flagged by some Dutch payroll systems and a small number of legacy direct debit setups. In 2026 it works for 95%+ of cases, but salary deposits remain occasionally problematic.

**Revolut:** Lithuanian IBAN (LT...) by default until late 2024. Since then, Dutch residents can opt into a real Dutch IBAN (NL...REVO...) which removes the friction entirely. If you opt in, Revolut now matches Bunq for Dutch IBAN convenience.

For the average expat in 2026, **Revolut with the Dutch IBAN is now the more friction-free choice** for everyday Dutch use. Wise still has friction but rarely catastrophic friction.

## Salary Deposits

I'd recommend neither as your primary salary destination, but if forced to pick:

**Revolut with Dutch IBAN:** works essentially as a Dutch bank does. Some employers may still raise an eyebrow at "Revolut Bank UAB" on the IBAN-to-name lookup, but technically it works.

**Wise:** the Belgian IBAN gets flagged occasionally. If your HR system runs an automated IBAN check, you may have to push back. Worth doing for the FX benefits if you're paid in non-EUR, but not worth the friction otherwise.

**My approach:** Dutch salary into Bunq or ABN AMRO. Excess transferred to Wise for non-EUR holding or to Revolut for travel and savings. Salary in a real Dutch bank, FX in Wise/Revolut.

## Multi-Currency Account

**Wise:** holds 50+ currencies natively, each with its own account number/IBAN/sort code. You can receive USD into a US-format account number, GBP into a UK sort code, EUR into a BE IBAN. Best-in-class for multi-currency receivers.

**Revolut:** holds ~30 currencies, but doesn't give you native local account numbers in most. You receive in a single IBAN and Revolut converts internally if you ask. Good for traveling and quick FX, weaker for receiving real foreign-currency payments.

Winner for receiving foreign currency: **Wise, by a clear margin.**

## Savings Rates

**Revolut Standard:** ~1–2% on EUR Savings Vaults
**Revolut Premium/Ultra:** up to ~3–3.5% in 2026
**Wise Interest:** ~3% on EUR balances (via Wise Assets, money market fund based, slight risk profile)

Both are okay but neither is best-in-class for savings. Bunq Easy Savings (~2.5–3.5%) and dedicated platforms like Trade Republic or Raisin beat both. Use Wise/Revolut savings only for short-term parking, not your main emergency fund.

## Travel Features

**Revolut wins this comprehensively.**

- Free EU/EEA ATM withdrawals (Standard: €200/month free; Premium: €400; Metal/Ultra: more)
- Travel insurance bundled in Premium and above
- Lounge passes on Metal/Ultra
- eSIM data plans across 100+ countries
- Trip-specific virtual cards
- Excellent metro/airport transit acceptance

**Wise's travel offering** is the debit card with mid-market FX rates. Solid but minimalist. Free ATM withdrawals are limited (~€200/month) and ATM fees apply beyond that.

For active travelers, Revolut's bundled features genuinely save money. For people who travel twice a year, Wise's card is enough.

## Investing and Stocks

**Revolut:** stocks (US fractional + EU stocks), ETFs, crypto, commodities. Fees are okay but not best-in-class — DEGIRO and Trade Republic beat Revolut for ETF buying. Reasonable for hobby investing, not for serious portfolios.

**Wise:** has Wise Assets (money market fund) for EUR/GBP/USD but doesn't offer stock investing. They've explicitly chosen not to add it.

For real investing, neither is the right tool. Use a dedicated broker. See my [Meesman vs DEGIRO](/blog/meesman-vs-degiro-investing-2026/) and [ETF platforms](/blog/etf-investing-platforms-netherlands-expats-2026/) guides.

## Customer Service

**Wise:** chat-based, 24/7 in English, response times typically 2–10 minutes. Above average among neobanks/fintechs.

**Revolut:** chat-only, Standard tier is slow (sometimes hours), Premium gets priority queue. Notorious for "your account is restricted, contact support" issues that take days to resolve. Major recurring complaint.

For lower-stakes use both are fine. For "my €5,000 transfer is stuck, I need it today" Wise wins.

## My Personal Split

Here's what I actually do:

**Wise** holds my EUR, GBP, and USD balances. I receive non-EUR client payments here. I transfer to other countries from here when sending more than €1,000. I keep €0–€500 floating for next-month foreign expenses.

**Revolut** is my travel and FX-active card. I top up before trips. I use the eSIM when I land outside the EU. I run small Vault savings for sinking funds (next holiday, gifts). I do not receive my salary here.

**Bunq** is my Dutch IBAN for salary, rent, utilities, gemeente direct debits. The actual "Dutch banking" account.

**Wise + Revolut + Bunq** is the expat finance trinity. Each does one thing better than the other two.

## Decision Framework

**Pick Wise if you:**
- Send international transfers regularly (£500+ a month to family or work)
- Receive money in multiple currencies (foreign clients, freelancing across countries)
- Value transparent pricing and predictable rates
- Travel a few times a year, not constantly
- Want one tool for cross-border money movement

**Pick Revolut if you:**
- Travel often
- Want a unified app for spending, savings, light investing
- Use crypto casually
- Want eSIM/lounge access bundled
- Need a reliable Dutch IBAN (with the new opt-in)

**Pick both if you:**
- Are a typical expat — Wise for transfers and FX-savings, Revolut for travel and Dutch IBAN. The combined cost on free tiers is €0/month.

## Common Wise vs Revolut Mistakes

**Sending big transfers via Revolut on a Saturday.** Costs you 1% extra. Wait until Monday or use Wise.

**Treating either as your primary Dutch bank.** Salary deposits, gemeente direct debits, and DUO disbursements work cleanest with a true Dutch bank. Use Wise/Revolut alongside, not instead.

**Ignoring the Dutch IBAN opt-in on Revolut.** If you're a Dutch resident and you haven't opted into the NL IBAN, do it now. Removes 90% of the historical friction.

**Paying for Revolut Premium without using the features.** €7.99/month is €96/year. If you don't travel often, fly twice a year max, and don't max the savings rates — stick with Standard.

**Trying to invest seriously on either.** Both are fine for play money. For real ETF investing use DEGIRO, Trade Republic, or Meesman.
