## The Three Routes to Insurance in NL

There are three fundamentally different ways an expat buys insurance:

**Route A: Direct from insurer.** Go to ASR, Centraal Beheer, ABN AMRO Verzekeringen directly via their website. Cheapest in absolute terms. You handle everything yourself in Dutch (mostly) or limited English.

**Route B: Comparison platform.** Use <a href="https://go.expatnetherlandshub.com/independer-zorg?ref=/blog/best-expat-insurance-brokers-netherlands-2026/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow sponsored noopener">Independer</a>, Pricewise, Hoyhoy. Compare offers across 10–20 insurers in English, click through to insurer for the actual policy. Self-service, cost-equivalent to direct, with massive time savings on comparison.

**Route C: Independent broker (tussenpersoon).** Personal advisor who shops across insurers, recommends products, manages claims, renews annually. Higher cost (5–15% premium markup via commissions, sometimes more visible fees for life/pension). Personalized advice, English service, hand-holding through complex claims.

The right route depends on the insurance type and your situation.

## Best Routes by Insurance Type

### Health Insurance — Comparison Platform Wins

Dutch basisverzekering is highly standardized by law — coverage is the same across insurers (premium and service vary). Comparison platforms like Independer show all 25+ insurers, in English, with the actual price differences. <a href="https://go.expatnetherlandshub.com/independer-zorg?ref=/blog/best-expat-insurance-brokers-netherlands-2026/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow sponsored noopener">Compare here</a>.

A broker adds little value for basisverzekering — they can't get you a different price than the insurer's standard rate.

For aanvullende (supplementary) health insurance, comparison platforms still work well. For premium expat-private-medical needs (Cigna, Bupa Global), an international broker may add value.

**Best route:** comparison platform for 95%+ of cases. Use a broker only if you want fully managed annual renewal hand-holding.

### Car Insurance — Comparison Platform Wins

Dutch car insurance is a competitive market — over 30 insurers offer WA (third-party) and All-Risk policies. Comparison platforms <a href="https://go.expatnetherlandshub.com/independer-auto?ref=/blog/best-expat-insurance-brokers-netherlands-2026/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow sponsored noopener">like Independer for car insurance</a> show real-time quotes for your specific car, age, no-claims discount, and address.

Brokers can sometimes find specialty policies (classic car, multi-driver, very high-value vehicles) but for everyday drivers they're not necessary.

**Best route:** comparison platform unless your situation is unusual.

### Home Contents (Inboedel) — Comparison Platform Wins

Standardized product, transparent pricing. <a href="https://go.expatnetherlandshub.com/independer-inboedel?ref=/blog/best-expat-insurance-brokers-netherlands-2026/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow sponsored noopener">Independer compares</a> all major insurers at typical premium €8–€20/month for renters' contents.

If you have very high-value contents (€50K+ artwork, jewelry, electronics), a broker can recommend a specialist policy with appropriate sub-limits.

**Best route:** comparison platform for renters and average-value households.

### Liability Insurance (Aansprakelijkheidsverzekering) — Comparison Platform

Standard product, €4–€8/month, comparable across all insurers. Can be bundled with home contents at most providers.

**Best route:** comparison platform.

### Travel Insurance — Comparison Platform or Bundled

Dutch travel insurance comes in annual continuous (~€60–€120/year) or trip-specific (€20–€50/trip) flavors. Often bundled into your bank account (Bunq Premium, Revolut Metal, ING premium pakketten). For frequent travelers, evaluate the bundle vs standalone — Revolut Metal includes worldwide travel insurance free.

**Best route:** comparison platform for standalone, or bundle with bank/card.

### Life Insurance (Overlijdensrisicoverzekering) — Broker Often Better

Life insurance is more complex (term length, sum insured, healthcare disclosures, partner co-coverage), and product nuances matter. A broker can structure a 30-year decreasing-sum policy aligned with your mortgage, or compare hybrid life-investment products.

Independer offers life insurance comparison too, but for sums above €250K or complex family situations, a broker's advice has real value.

**Best route:** broker for sums >€250K or complex setups; comparison platform for simple term life under €250K.

### Disability Insurance (AOV) for ZZP — Broker Strongly Recommended

AOV is one of the most complex Dutch insurance products — coverage definitions vary widely between insurers, premium €100–€350/month, and the wrong product can fail you when you need it most. Brokers specializing in ZZP AOV (e.g., De Goudse, Movir, ASR) earn their commission for this product.

**Best route:** specialist broker.

### Mortgage-Linked Insurances — Broker

When buying a house, the mortgage broker (hypotheekadviseur) typically bundles the linked insurances — life insurance, mortgage protection, optional content insurance. They have a fiduciary obligation under MiFID II to disclose fees explicitly.

**Best route:** mortgage broker handles this as part of the mortgage process.

### International / Expat-Specific Coverage — International Broker

Premium private medical insurance (Cigna, Bupa Global, Allianz Worldwide Care), international school fee insurance, multi-country property coverage — these aren't products that comparison platforms or domestic Dutch brokers handle well. <a href="https://go.expatnetherlandshub.com/cigna?ref=/blog/best-expat-insurance-brokers-netherlands-2026/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow sponsored noopener">Cigna</a> is the most established international expat health insurance.

**Best route:** international broker or direct.

## When You Definitely Need a Broker

Sign up with a broker if any of these apply:

**1. You're ZZP / self-employed.** AOV (disability), bedrijfsaansprakelijkheid (business liability), and beroepsaansprakelijkheid (professional liability) need expert structuring. See [ZZP registration guide](/blog/zzp-registration-netherlands-step-by-step-2026/).

**2. You're buying a house.** Mortgage-linked insurances, structural building insurance, and the optional opstal-vs-inboedel split need a broker's eye.

**3. You have international coverage needs.** Multi-country property, premium private medical, expat schools.

**4. You have high-value assets.** €100K+ in jewelry, art, electronics, classic cars — require specialist sub-limit structuring.

**5. You don't speak Dutch and don't want to navigate Dutch claims processes.** A broker handles this.

**6. You've had a complex claim before and want managed renewal.** Brokers smooth renewals after claims that might otherwise spike your premiums.

## When You Definitely Don't Need a Broker

Stick with comparison platforms if:

**1. You're a single renter with simple finances.** Health, contents, liability, maybe car. All comparison-platform-friendly.

**2. You're cost-sensitive and self-service oriented.** A few hours of comparison saves €100–€200/year.

**3. Your expat status is short-term (under 3 years).** Setting up a broker relationship for short-term coverage isn't worth the friction.

**4. You're tech-comfortable and your English-only insurance needs are basic.** Independer's English service covers this thoroughly.

## Common Insurance Broker Mistakes Expats Make

**Hiring a broker for everything.** Pays unnecessary markups on simple insurance like home contents (10% extra for service that's not needed).

**Using the broker the previous tenant recommended without comparing.** Some brokers are amazing, some are mediocre. Get one or two quotes from comparison platforms to sanity-check broker pricing.

**Not asking about commissions and fees.** A reputable broker should explain how they're paid. If they're evasive, switch.

**Sticking with the broker after they're not adding value.** Annual review yourself: am I paying €100/year more for service I don't use? If yes, switch to comparison platform.

**Not engaging a broker for ZZP AOV.** Single biggest mistake I see. ZZP'ers either skip AOV entirely (catastrophic if they get sick) or buy bad AOV products from comparison sites without understanding the coverage definitions.

**Buying premium international health insurance "just in case."** If you're committed to NL 5+ years and use the Dutch system, basisverzekering + aanvullend is sufficient. Premium international insurance is for global mobility, not for "extra coverage" you'll never use.

**Forgetting the annual review.** Both comparison-platform users and broker clients should re-shop major coverage annually during November (the switching window for health insurance, and a natural time to revisit other policies).

## How to Pick a Broker

When evaluating a broker:

1. **English-speaking confirmed.** Ask explicitly. Many brokers say "yes" but mean "I can manage emails." Talk on the phone first.
2. **Independent / multi-insurer.** A tied agent only sells one insurer's products and has worse incentives than a true independent broker.
3. **Transparent on fees and commissions.** Should be willing to explain compensation structure.
4. **Specialty matches your needs.** ZZP-focused, expat-focused, family-focused — not a generalist.
5. **Personal referrals.** Best signal of quality — ask 2–3 expats in similar situations.
6. **Test their claims handling.** Make a small claim once a year (e.g., scratched phone screen) — see how they handle it. Slow or evasive claims handling is the #1 reason to switch brokers.
