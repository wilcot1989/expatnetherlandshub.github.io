I've held N26, Bunq, and Revolut accounts simultaneously for two-plus years, switched my primary account between them twice, and helped a dozen expat friends figure out which one to choose first when they arrive. The honest answer is that the three are not really competing for the same role — once you understand what each is best at, the comparison stops being "which one wins" and becomes "which combination fits your life."

This guide covers the 2026 reality of using N26, Bunq, and Revolut as an expat in the Netherlands: real fees, IBAN issues that actually matter, savings rates, app quality, and the decision framework I use when friends ask.

## What Each Bank Actually Is

**Bunq** is a Dutch bank, founded in Amsterdam in 2012 by Ali Niknam. Full Dutch banking license, supervised by DNB. Around 17 million customers across Europe in 2026. Its identity is "the bank of the free" — heavy on personal customization, sub-accounts, sustainability features. Headquartered in Amsterdam, mobile-first.

**N26** is a German neobank founded in 2013 in Berlin. Full German banking license, supervised by BaFin. Around 8 million customers. Its identity is clean simplicity — a polished mobile-first banking experience with strong UX, available across most of the EU.

**Revolut** is a UK-origin financial services company that holds a Lithuanian banking license through Revolut Bank UAB. Around 50 million customers globally. Its identity is "all-in-one financial super-app" — banking, investing, crypto, insurance, travel — under one roof.

## IBAN Reality in the Netherlands

This is where the three diverge most for Dutch use. SEPA rules technically prohibit IBAN discrimination, but practical Dutch system integration varies.

**Bunq:** Dutch IBAN (NL.. BUNQ ..). Recognized everywhere — payroll, Belastingdienst, utilities, all Dutch SEPA direct debits.

**N26:** German IBAN (DE.. NTSB ..). SEPA-compliant in principle, but some Dutch employers' payroll systems and some legacy direct-debit setups still occasionally reject non-NL IBANs. Has improved over the years; rare problem in 2026 but not zero.

**Revolut:** Lithuanian IBAN (LT.. REVO ..) by default. Same friction as N26 — usually fine, occasionally rejected. Revolut began offering Dutch IBANs to Dutch residents in late 2024; if you can opt for the NL IBAN, do so.

For salary deposits, Bunq is friction-free. N26 and Revolut work for most employers but you may have to push back if HR initially rejects. Switch your salary IBAN to the new account on day one of a new job to avoid mid-cycle issues.

## Account Opening for Expats

All three accept expats without major hassle.

**Bunq:** Need a passport/EU ID, address (Dutch or other EU), and selfie video verification. Free 30-day trial. After trial, you choose a paid plan (no truly free Dutch tier). BSN requested for Dutch residents but not required at signup.

**N26:** Passport/EU ID, address, video selfie verification. Free Standard plan available across most EU. Verification takes 10–30 minutes.

**Revolut:** ID, address, selfie verification. Free Standard plan available. Most flexible for new arrivals — accepts almost any EU residence and EU ID.

**For BSN-less new arrivals:** Revolut and N26 are easier to open day one. Bunq is open-able but you'll need BSN within a few months. See [BSN application guide](/blog/expat-bsn-application-guide-2026/).

## FX and Travel — Where Differences Show

This is the most-asked question I get from expats traveling internationally or sending money abroad.

**Revolut:** Mid-market FX with weekend markup (~0.5–1%). Free conversion up to monthly limits depending on plan; fees apply over. 0.5%–1% fee on currency conversions outside major pairs.

**N26:** Mid-market FX, no fee on EUR transactions. Foreign currency card transactions use Mastercard FX (close to mid-market). 1.7% foreign ATM fee on Standard, free on paid plans up to limits.

**Bunq:** Real-time FX at mid-market rates. Foreign card transactions free up to limits depending on plan tier.

For pure FX volume (sending, say, €10,000 to a foreign account), [Wise](https://go.expatnetherlandshub.com/wise?ref=/blog/n26-vs-bunq-vs-revolut-expats-netherlands-2026/) typically beats all three on transparent fees and rate. Wise isn't a checking account but pairs perfectly with any of these for outbound international transfers.

For travel card use (paying with your card abroad), all three are solid. Revolut has the most features (travel insurance, lounge access on premium tiers).

## Savings Interest in 2026

Rates have shifted significantly since 2022. Mid-2026 status (rates change frequently — verify current):

**Bunq:** Easy Savings tier offers up to 2.5–3.5% on savings sub-accounts. Capped to certain balance amounts on lower tiers.

**Revolut:** Premium offers ~2.5% in EUR savings vaults. Ultra offers slightly higher. Standard tier offers minimal interest.

**N26:** Up to 2.5% on Metal tier through the "Instant Savings" product. Standard tier offers minimal interest.

For pure savings yield, none of these beat dedicated savings products like Trade Republic's cash interest (often 3–4% in 2024–2026), or Raisin marketplace deposits (3–4.5%). Use neobank savings for short-term cash; use specialist savings products for larger amounts.

## App Quality and User Experience

This is the most subjective category. After two years of daily use of all three:

**N26:** The cleanest, fastest, most intuitive app. Minimal feature creep. Excellent for people who want banking to feel boring and reliable.

**Revolut:** The most features. Banking, investing, crypto, insurance, travel deals, group expenses, junior accounts — all in one app. Powerful but cluttered. I find I use ~20% of features and the rest is noise.

**Bunq:** Feature-rich with strong personalization. Sub-accounts (Easy Bank+), automatic savings rules, shared accounts, budgeting widgets. Steeper learning curve than N26 but more powerful once you set it up.

**For pure UX simplicity:** N26.
**For all-in-one features:** Revolut.
**For Dutch-banking-power-user setup:** Bunq.

## Customer Service in English

All three offer English customer service.

**Bunq:** In-app chat and email. English support is solid. Phone support requires premium tiers.

**N26:** In-app chat and email. English support is fast and clear. Premium tiers get priority support.

**Revolut:** In-app chat. English support is hit-or-miss — sometimes excellent, sometimes long waits or scripted responses.

For complex issues (chargebacks, fraud, account locks), Bunq has the most consistent experience for me. Revolut has the most stories of frustrating service interactions in expat communities.

## My Recommendation Framework

**For a brand-new expat in their first 30 days:** Open Revolut Standard for immediate use (no BSN needed) plus Bunq Easy Bank during your free trial period. Use Revolut for FX/travel and Bunq for setting up Dutch payroll and bills once you have BSN.

**For a settled expat with Dutch salary:** Bunq Easy Bank or Easy Savings is your daily account. Optionally add Revolut Standard or Premium for travel.

**For a freelance expat:** Bunq business account + Wise multi-currency for international clients. N26 or Revolut as a personal-spending sidecar if you want.

**For a high-balance expat (€50K+ in cash):** Use Bunq for daily, Trade Republic/Raisin for savings yield, and a traditional Dutch bank for redundancy. Spread €100K+ across institutions for deposit insurance.

## Card Quality and Acceptance

All three offer Mastercard or Visa debit cards accepted globally where the relevant network operates.

**Bunq:** Mastercard debit standard; metal card on premium tiers. Multiple physical cards per account possible (linked to different sub-accounts). Apple Pay and Google Pay supported.

**N26:** Mastercard debit standard; metal card on Metal tier. One physical card per account. Apple Pay, Google Pay, Garmin Pay supported.

**Revolut:** Mastercard or Visa (varies). Multiple cards possible per account. Disposable virtual cards for online security. Apple Pay, Google Pay, Garmin Pay, Fitbit Pay, Samsung Pay supported.

For Dutch domestic use, all three are widely accepted. Watch out: some Dutch retailers (especially smaller shops, market vendors) only accept iDEAL or Maestro and may not take all card types — though this is reducing. iDEAL bank-payment integration works with Bunq native; with N26 and Revolut, iDEAL works through their respective in-app payment flows.

## ATM Access in the Netherlands

Cash matters less than it used to in the Netherlands but isn't zero.

**Bunq:** Free ATM withdrawals at most Dutch ATMs depending on plan tier. Limits vary.

**N26:** Free EUR ATM withdrawals up to limits depending on plan. Standard tier: 3 free per month, then €2 per withdrawal.

**Revolut:** Free ATM withdrawals up to €200/month on Standard, higher on Premium and Metal.

For typical expats withdrawing cash 1–3 times per month for small purchases or split bills, all three are adequate. Heavy cash users may prefer traditional Dutch banks or higher-tier neobank plans.

## Joint Accounts and Shared Finances

If you live with a partner and want shared account capability:

**Bunq:** Best joint account experience. Easy to set up shared "spaces" with role-based access (full access, view only, etc.). Multiple linked cards per shared space.

**N26:** Spaces feature allows shared access with limitations.

**Revolut:** Group bills feature for splitting expenses, joint account features more limited than Bunq.

For couples managing rent, utilities, and shared expenses, Bunq's shared sub-accounts are the cleanest setup of the three.

## Customer Service Friction Points

Real-world friction patterns I've encountered or heard from expat friends:

**Account locks.** Revolut has a reputation for sudden account locks during automated fraud reviews — sometimes resolved in hours, sometimes in days. Bunq and N26 are less prone to this but not immune.

**Chargebacks.** All three handle chargebacks reasonably. Bunq and N26 are slightly better at communication during the process.

**Lost or stolen card replacement.** All three issue replacement cards within 5–10 business days domestically. Revolut and Bunq instantly provide virtual card numbers in-app while waiting for physical replacement.

**Transfer reversals.** N26 and Bunq are responsive on documented disputed transfers. Revolut's approach is more automated, sometimes leaving customers in limbo.

For risk-averse banking (your primary salary account, mortgage payments), I personally trust Bunq most. For travel and FX, Revolut is great even with its quirks. N26 sits in between as a clean general-purpose account.

## Cards for Children / Junior Accounts

For expat families:

**Revolut:** Junior accounts (Revolut <18) with parental oversight, spending controls, savings goals. Solid feature set.

**Bunq:** Sub-account for children with parental controls, increasingly mature feature.

**N26:** Limited junior offering.

For parents wanting to give kids financial responsibility under monitored conditions, Revolut Junior is the strongest of the three.

## Crypto, Stocks, and Commodities

This is Revolut's territory:

**Revolut:** In-app crypto, stocks, ETFs, commodities. Convenient but not transferable to external wallets/brokers. Spreads on crypto are higher than dedicated exchanges. For real serious investing, use [DEGIRO or Trade Republic](/blog/degiro-vs-trade-republic-expats-2026/) instead.

**Bunq:** No native investing. Cash management only.

**N26:** Limited crypto trading partnership; stocks via separate "N26 Crypto" or partner integration.

If you want investing within your banking app, Revolut wins for convenience. If you want serious investing, look outside the neobanks entirely.

## Privacy and Data Considerations

All three are GDPR-compliant European banks. Practical privacy differences:

**Bunq** has the most explicit privacy-first marketing, with options like "no third-party data sharing" by default.

**N26** is privacy-respecting but uses third-party analytics for app improvement.

**Revolut** has had occasional data-handling controversies; nothing showstopping but more concerning history than the others.

For typical expats this is a minor consideration; for privacy-focused users, Bunq's positioning aligns best.

## Brand Stability and Long-Term Outlook

All three are profitable and well-funded as of 2026, but with different risk profiles:

**Bunq:** Profitable, growing, focused on Europe. Lower risk of dramatic strategy shifts.

**N26:** Profitable in core markets, has historically pulled out of some markets (UK, US) when not profitable. Has cleaned up post-2022 BaFin compliance issues.

**Revolut:** Highly profitable, growing rapidly, but with frequent product launches, occasional regulatory friction, and broader strategic ambitions (lending, super-app). More moving parts.

For 5–10 year horizons, all three look stable enough to be reliable. None should be your only bank — diversification across institutions is sensible above €100K total liquid assets.

## My Annual Review Process

Every January I revisit:

1. **Whose savings rate is highest** for my balance level — sometimes a switch saves €100–€500/year.
2. **Whose travel insurance covers my actual trips** — Premium-tier travel insurance varies meaningfully on what's actually included.
3. **Whose features I'm not using** — am I paying €10/month for something I haven't touched?
4. **Whose customer service experience** has been good or bad in the past year.

This 30-minute exercise has saved me €200–€400/year on banking fees over the past 3 years. Worth the time.
