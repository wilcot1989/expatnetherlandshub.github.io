## How to File Online (Regular Return)

### What You Need
- **DigiD** — Your digital identity for government services
- **Annual statement (jaaropgave)** from your employer
- **WOZ value** of your home (if owner)
- **Mortgage details** (if applicable)
- **Bank account balances** as of January 1 (for Box 3)
- **Health insurance costs** (if claiming deduction)
- **Donation receipts** (for ANBI charities)

### Step-by-Step

**Step 1:** Go to [mijn.belastingdienst.nl](https://mijn.belastingdienst.nl) and log in with DigiD

**Step 2:** Select "Aangifte inkomstenbelasting 2025"

**Step 3:** Review pre-filled data — the tax authority pre-fills much of your return from employer and bank data. Check everything carefully.

**Step 4:** Complete additional sections:
- Box 1: Income from work and home (salary, mortgage deduction)
- Box 2: Income from substantial interest (5%+ company shares)
- Box 3: Savings and investments (taxed on assumed return)

**Step 5:** Review deductions (see below)

**Step 6:** Submit and save your confirmation

### The Three Tax Boxes

| Box | What's Taxed | Tax Rate |
|-----|-------------|---------|
| **Box 1** | Salary, pension, business income, own home | 36.97% (up to €75,518) / 49.50% (above) |
| **Box 2** | Dividend and gains from substantial interest (≥5%) | 24.5% (up to €67,000) / 33% (above) |
| **Box 3** | Savings, investments, property (not own home) | 36% on assumed return (1.03%-6.04%) |

## The M-Form (First/Last Year)

The M-form is required for the year you arrived in or left the Netherlands. It's more complex because it covers:
- Your Dutch income for the period you lived in NL
- Your worldwide income for the full year (for determining tax rates)
- Choice between resident and non-resident taxpayer status

### Important M-Form Facts
- Cannot be filed online — download from belastingdienst.nl or request by phone
- Deadline: same as regular return (May 1 or extended)
- Strongly recommended to use a tax advisor
- You can choose the most favorable taxpayer status

### Resident vs Non-Resident Status
| Status | When | Box 3 on worldwide assets? | Dutch deductions? |
|--------|------|--------------------------|-------------------|
| **Resident (binnenlands)** | Lived in NL most of the year | Yes | Yes, all |
| **Non-resident (buitenlands)** | Lived outside NL most of the year | No, NL only | Limited |
| **Partial non-resident** (with 30% ruling) | 30% ruling active | No (Box 3 exempt) | Limited |

## 30% Ruling and Your Tax Return

If you have the 30% ruling:

### What Changes
1. Only 70% of your gross salary is taxable
2. You can opt for "partial non-resident" status
3. Box 3: Only taxed on Dutch assets (not worldwide savings)
4. Some deductions are limited or unavailable

### Partial Non-Resident Status
This is often the biggest tax advantage of the 30% ruling:
- Your foreign bank accounts, investments, and property are **exempt from Box 3 tax**
- Only Dutch bank accounts and Dutch property are taxed in Box 3
- Choose this status on your tax return (it's not automatic!)

## Common Deductions for Expats

| Deduction | What | Potential Savings |
|-----------|------|-----------------|
| **Mortgage interest** | Interest on your Dutch mortgage | €1,000-€5,000/year |
| **Pension (lijfrente)** | Private pension contributions | Up to €15,000/year deductible |
| **Charitable donations** | To ANBI-registered charities | 1-10% of income |
| **Healthcare costs** | Unreimbursed medical expenses above threshold | Varies |
| **Study costs** | Professional education (limited) | Up to €15,000 |
| **Specific care costs** | Disability, chronic illness | Varies |

### Mortgage Interest Deduction
The biggest deduction for homeowners:
- Deduct all mortgage interest on your primary residence
- Must have a repayment mortgage (not interest-only for new mortgages)
- Add WOZ value as taxable income (eigenwoningforfait)
- Net benefit: typically €1,000-€5,000/year in tax savings

## Common Mistakes

1. **Not filing the M-form** — Required for arrival/departure year
2. **Forgetting partial non-resident status** — Must actively choose it
3. **Missing mortgage deduction** — File even if not invited
4. **Wrong Box 3 declaration** — Report worldwide assets (unless partial non-resident)
5. **Missing the deadline** — File or request extension before May 1
6. **Not checking pre-filled data** — The tax authority's data can be incomplete
